## 1. CIA -- "Clowns in America" in Conspiracy Culture

Within QAnon and adjacent conspiracy communities, the CIA is referred to as **"Clowns in America"** (sometimes "Clowns in Action"). The term originated in Q drops -- the cryptic posts by the anonymous figure "Q" -- where "clowns" served as a codeword for CIA agents and intelligence operatives allegedly working to suppress Q's mission and protect the so-called "deep state."

The broader QAnon narrative frames the CIA as a rogue agency that operates independently of elected officials, conducts illegal domestic operations, and serves a globalist cabal rather than American citizens. This builds on decades of actual CIA controversies -- MKUltra, Operation Mockingbird, Iran-Contra -- which conspiracy theorists extrapolate far beyond documented facts into claims of total media control, assassination programs, and drug trafficking.

The "Clowns" label functions as in-group shorthand: it simultaneously mocks the agency and signals membership in the QAnon community. Former CIA operations have been documented in declassified files, but the QAnon narrative treats the agency as a monolithic villain in a struggle between good (Trump, white hats) and evil (the cabal).

## 2. NSA / FBI / SES -- Surveillance State Conspiracy

This cluster of conspiracy claims ties together the **National Security Agency (NSA)**, the **Federal Bureau of Investigation (FBI)**, and the **Senior Executive Service (SES)** into a unified "surveillance state" narrative.

**The SES Conspiracy:** The Senior Executive Service was created in 1979 under President Carter as a corps of senior federal managers. In conspiracy circles -- particularly after 2017 -- the SES became labeled as "the real deep state." Proponents claim that over 7,000 of its roughly 8,000 members were appointed during the Obama administration and function as entrenched "holdovers" who resist presidential authority and execute a shadow agenda. In reality, the SES is a standard civil service classification governed by law, and its members serve across administrations of both parties.

**NSA Surveillance:** Unlike pure conspiracy, NSA mass surveillance became documented fact after Edward Snowden's 2013 leaks revealed programs like PRISM, which collected user data from major tech companies. The documented scope of NSA operations -- intercepting and storing communications of over a billion people worldwide -- gave enormous credibility to surveillance-state fears that had previously been dismissed as paranoia.

**FBI Concerns:** The FBI's history of domestic surveillance (COINTELPRO in the 1960s-70s, modern Section 702 authorities) similarly blurs the line between documented misconduct and speculative conspiracy.

The Great Awakening Map conflates these documented programs with unproven claims of a coordinated, permanent bureaucratic conspiracy against the American people.

## 3. Council of 200 -- Alleged Shadow Government

The "Council of 200" on the Great Awakening Map likely refers to or conflates with the **Committee of 300**, a conspiracy theory popularized by British author John Coleman in his 1991 book *Conspirators' Hierarchy: The Story of the Committee of 300*.

Coleman claimed the Committee of 300 (also called "the Olympians") is a secret society of the world's most powerful individuals -- drawn from royalty, finance, business, and politics -- that forms a hidden world government controlling the fate of nations. He alleged that Queen Elizabeth II headed the Committee, that U.S. Presidents were members, and that institutions like the Council on Foreign Relations, the Royal Institute of International Affairs, and the Club of Rome served as its front organizations. The book's ultimate claim is that the Committee's goal is total genocide of "useless eaters," leaving only a small surviving elite.

Notably, a copy of Coleman's book was found in Osama bin Laden's Abbottabad compound by the CIA, which has made the document publicly available. Critics describe the book as an unfocused collection of accusations, including bizarre claims such as the Rolling Stones being created as a mind-control cult.

The broader "shadow government" conspiracy family includes allegations about the Bilderberg Group, Trilateral Commission, Illuminati, Freemasons, and various banking dynasties. These narratives share a common structure: a small elite secretly controls world events behind a facade of democracy.

## 4. Surveillance Grid -- Mass Surveillance Conspiracy

The "Surveillance Grid" concept on the map refers to the belief that governments have built (or are building) a seamless, all-encompassing system of monitoring that tracks every person's movements, communications, purchases, and behavior.

**What is documented:** The **Five Eyes** alliance (US, UK, Australia, Canada, New Zealand) was formed in 1946 and, as revealed by Edward Snowden in 2013, operates numerous joint surveillance programs. The **PRISM** program collected user data directly from Google, Apple, Microsoft, Facebook, and other tech companies. The **Upstream** system tapped fiber-optic cables to intercept communications in transit. Privacy International has noted that Five Eyes intelligence-sharing arrangements are "shrouded in secrecy" and allow "arbitrary or unlawful intrusions on the right to privacy which circumvent domestic legal restrictions."

**What is speculative:** Conspiracy versions extend these documented facts into claims of a total surveillance grid involving facial recognition at every corner, real-time tracking of all citizens, AI-powered behavioral prediction, and a social credit system modeled on China's. While individual surveillance technologies do exist, the claim that they form a unified, deliberately constructed "grid" for population control goes beyond available evidence.

The tension here is real: documented surveillance programs were so far beyond what officials publicly admitted that the boundary between "conspiracy theory" and "investigative journalism" shifted dramatically after 2013.

## 5. Corporatocracy -- Corporate Rule Concept

**Corporatocracy** (also "corpocracy") refers to an economic, political, and judicial system controlled or heavily influenced by corporate interests. Unlike many items on the Great Awakening Map, corporatocracy is a concept with substantial academic and political science lineage -- it is discussed by mainstream economists, political theorists, and critics of globalization.

The term has been used to describe phenomena including bank bailouts, excessive CEO compensation, corporate lobbying that shapes legislation, exploitation of natural resources in developing nations, and the revolving door between government regulators and the industries they regulate. Scholars identify variants including crony capitalism, authoritarian capitalism, and "inverted totalitarianism" (a term coined by political theorist Sheldon Wolin).

In conspiracy culture, corporatocracy is framed more dramatically: corporations are not merely influential but are the actual ruling power, with elected governments serving as a facade. This connects to "deep state" narratives where unelected corporate and financial elites set policy regardless of election outcomes. The Great Awakening Map positions corporatocracy as part of a deliberately constructed system of control rather than an emergent consequence of political economy.

The distinction matters: mainstream critics argue corporatocracy results from systemic incentives and power dynamics, while conspiracy narratives treat it as evidence of a coordinated, secret plan.

## 6. "1984" -- Orwell's Relevance to Conspiracy Theories

George Orwell's 1949 novel *Nineteen Eighty-Four* has become the single most referenced literary work in conspiracy culture. Its concepts -- **Big Brother**, **Newspeak**, **doublethink**, **thoughtcrime**, the **Memory Hole**, and the **Two Minutes Hate** -- function as a shared vocabulary for describing perceived government overreach and social manipulation.

According to the Smithsonian, the three major themes of continuing relevance are: "the denial of objective truth," "the manipulation of language and the use of words to bamboozle people," and "the rise of the surveillance society." The novel's sales have spiked repeatedly during political crises, including after the Snowden revelations and during the Trump administration.

In conspiracy circles, *1984* is treated not merely as a warning but as a **blueprint** -- a claim that governments and corporations have deliberately adopted Orwell's fictional strategies. The Conspiracy Theory Database states that "Big Brother, Newspeak, and the Two Minutes Hate are seen as literal strategies deployed in real life, from mass surveillance to algorithmic censorship," with some arguing the book is "an instruction manual, not just a novel."

Scholars like Shoshana Zuboff (author of *The Age of Surveillance Capitalism*) have argued that Orwell correctly anticipated state surveillance but missed the threat from private-sector surveillance capitalism -- a danger he could not have foreseen.

## 7. Flat Earth Psyop -- Conspiracy About Conspiracy

The "Flat Earth Psyop" is a meta-conspiracy: the theory that **flat earth belief itself is a deliberate psychological operation** designed to discredit all conspiracy theories by association. This is a conspiracy theory about a conspiracy theory.

The argument runs as follows: intelligence agencies (usually the CIA) intentionally promote obviously absurd beliefs like flat earth to create "conspiracy fatigue" in the public. When legitimate questions about government misconduct arise, they can be dismissed by association with flat-earthers. Proponents note that flat earth content experienced an estimated 600% increase in online activity starting around 2014, which they find suspiciously rapid.

The counter-narrative is documented across conspiracy forums and alternative media sites. One site argues flat earth is "a fraud, specifically designed to infiltrate and take over the CT [conspiracy theory] communities to distract and ultimately attempt to destroy the credibility of all conspiracy theories." Academic researchers have noted the circular logic: "believers of a less ridiculous conspiracy theory often wrongly claim that a more ridiculous one, like flat Earth, is a psyop or controlled opposition."

Research published in *The Conversation* found that beliefs like flat earth spread because they "appeal to a sense of group identity under attack" -- the same psychological mechanism that drives the meta-conspiracy about flat earth being a psyop.

## 8. Staged Mass Shootings -- False Flag Conspiracy Theory

The false flag mass shooting conspiracy theory claims that some or all mass shootings in the United States are **staged events** orchestrated by the government (or shadowy actors) to justify gun control legislation and erode the Second Amendment.

The most prominent case is the **Sandy Hook Elementary School shooting** (December 14, 2012), in which 20 children and 6 staff members were killed. Conspiracy theorist Alex Jones of InfoWars promoted the claim that the shooting was "fake," that "no one died," and that the victims were "child actors." A 2016 Fairleigh Dickinson University poll found that 22% of Americans believed it was "possibly or definitely true" that the Sandy Hook massacre was faked to increase support for gun control.

The real-world consequences have been severe. Families of victims testified that Jones' claims led to sustained harassment and death threats from conspiracy believers who accused them of faking their children's deaths. In 2022, a Connecticut jury ordered Jones to pay nearly $1 billion in damages to the families. In a 2019 deposition, Jones himself acknowledged the deaths were real, stating he had experienced "almost like a form of psychosis" in which he "basically thought everything was staged."

Experts trace the marriage of false flag narratives and mass shootings specifically to Sandy Hook in 2012, though the broader "false flag" concept -- governments staging attacks as pretexts for policy changes -- has older roots.

## 9. Crisis Actor -- Conspiracy Concept

A **crisis actor** in its legitimate meaning is a trained role player hired for emergency response drills to simulate disaster victims for first-responder training. In conspiracy culture, the term has been co-opted to claim that survivors and witnesses of mass casualty events are actually **paid actors** performing in staged events.

The conspiracy use of "crisis actor" originated in 2012 after Sandy Hook, when former professor and conspiracy theorist **James Tracy** published blog posts suggesting the government may have hired an acting agency called Visionbox to stage the shooting. His posts were amplified by InfoWars and Alex Jones, and the concept rapidly became a standard element of the conspiracy toolkit.

The theory has since been applied to survivors of the Parkland shooting (2018), the Boston Marathon bombing (2013), and numerous other tragedies. Survivors who speak publicly -- particularly student activists from Parkland -- have been accused of being crisis actors, leading to harassment and threats.

The SPLC (Southern Poverty Law Center) has documented how the "crisis actors" conspiracy theory has spread across the radical right. The theory serves a specific psychological function: it allows believers to dismiss evidence of mass violence that might challenge their worldview (particularly regarding gun policy) by reframing victims as participants in a hoax.

## 10. Weaponized Food / GMOs -- Conspiracy Theories

The "weaponized food" conspiracy cluster alleges that genetically modified organisms (GMOs) are deliberately designed to harm populations, reduce fertility, cause disease, or serve as tools of corporate domination over the global food supply.

**Key conspiracy narratives include:**

- **Depopulation:** Claims that the Gates Foundation, Rockefeller Foundation, and corporations like Monsanto (now Bayer) are using GMO seeds to reduce Africa's population and control its food supply.
- **Biological weapons:** Assertions that GMO technology transfers bacterial and viral genes into food to harm citizens of other countries.
- **Corporate monopoly:** Claims that Monsanto has suppressed data showing GMOs cause harm, deliberately causes food shortages to promote GMO adoption, and has co-opted regulatory agencies like the FDA.
- **Bill Gates connection:** Social media narratives linking Gates' agricultural investments to COVID-19, vaccines, and population control.

**Scientific consensus:** Following over 15 years of research, major scientific bodies (the National Academies of Sciences, the WHO, the American Medical Association) have concluded that approved GMO foods are safe for human consumption. The Genetic Literacy Project notes that the key claims of the anti-GMO movement are "not just wrong in points of fact, but in large parts the precise opposite of the truth."

Real concerns about corporate consolidation in agriculture (e.g., seed patents, farmer dependency) exist but are distinct from the conspiracy claim that food is being deliberately "weaponized."

## 11. Kill Your TV -- Media Manipulation Concept

"Kill Your TV" is a countercultural slogan that has migrated from anti-consumerist activism into conspiracy culture as shorthand for the belief that **television is a tool of deliberate mass mind control**.

The anti-television movement has legitimate roots. Jerry Mander's 1978 book *Four Arguments for the Elimination of Television* argued that the medium's structure inherently favors corporate and authoritarian messaging. Organizations like **Adbusters** (founded by Kalle Lasn) and the UK group **White Dot** organized community TV turnoffs and critiqued television's role in promoting consumerism, social atomization, and political passivity. The movement led to annual events like "TV-Turnoff Week" (later "Screen-Free Week").

In conspiracy culture, the concept is more extreme. Believers claim television intentionally produces a hypnotic state through screen flicker rates, that news programming is scripted propaganda coordinated between government and media corporations (an extension of "Operation Mockingbird" claims), and that television is designed to keep populations docile and compliant -- "programming" the viewer rather than the television.

The Waking Times site argues that television "offers corporations a surefire method of expanding their markets and controlling social and political thought." While legitimate media criticism exists, the conspiracy version treats all mainstream media as a monolithic, coordinated deception apparatus.

## 12. Sheep Asleep -- "Sheeple" Conspiracy Culture

**"Sheeple"** (a portmanteau of "sheep" and "people") and the associated phrase **"wake up"** form the core metaphorical framework of conspiracy culture. Those who accept mainstream narratives are "sheep" who are "asleep"; those who have discovered hidden truths are "awake" or "awakened."

This binary maps directly onto the **"red pill / blue pill"** metaphor from *The Matrix* (1999). Taking the "red pill" means choosing to see the hidden reality; the "blue pill" means remaining in comfortable ignorance. As one analysis notes: "Unlike the 'blue pills' of false consciousness that must be constantly fed to the 'sheeple' to keep them asleep, the red pill needs only be taken once."

The term "sheeple" gained traction in the early 2000s on conspiracy forums. In 2002, the blog *The Forbidden Knowledge* published an open letter addressed to "the sheeple" about the New World Order. By 2003, multiple definitions appeared on Urban Dictionary. The concept has since been adopted across the political spectrum but is most associated with far-right, QAnon, and men's rights activist communities.

The "Great Awakening" itself -- the name of the map -- is this metaphor writ large: a mass "awakening" of the population to hidden truths. The term deliberately echoes the historical Great Awakenings (religious revival movements in American history), framing conspiracy belief as a spiritual and intellectual liberation.

## 13. Dark Web -- Actual Technology + Conspiracy

The **dark web** is a real part of the internet that requires specialized software (typically the **Tor browser**) to access. It consists of websites with .onion addresses that are not indexed by standard search engines. The technology was originally developed by the U.S. Naval Research Laboratory for secure communications.

**Factual aspects:**
- The dark web hosts both legitimate and criminal activity. Legitimate uses include political dissident communications, whistleblower platforms (e.g., SecureDrop), and privacy-focused services. Major organizations like ProPublica and Facebook maintain .onion sites.
- Criminal activity includes drug marketplaces (e.g., the former Silk Road), arms trafficking, stolen data sales, and exploitation material.
- Most cybercrime actually occurs on the regular ("clear") web, not the dark web.

**Conspiracy and myth:**
- **Red Rooms** -- websites allegedly offering live-streamed torture and murder -- are widely considered urban legends rather than documented phenomena.
- Some conspiracy theorists believe the U.S. government controls Tor and has backdoors into all dark web activity, though the code is open source and has been audited by independent researchers.
- The dark web has itself become a vector for spreading conspiracy theories, misinformation, and forged documents.

The dark web occupies an unusual position on the Great Awakening Map: it is a real technology with documented criminal uses, but it is also surrounded by exaggerated myths that serve the map's narrative of hidden, sinister forces operating beneath the surface of normal life.

## 14. Rh- Blood Type -- Conspiracy Theories

The **Rh-negative blood type** conspiracy theory claims that people with Rh-negative blood are descendants of **extraterrestrial beings**, **the biblical Nephilim** (angel-human hybrids), or the **Anunnaki** (an alleged alien race from ancient Mesopotamian mythology).

**The theory's logic:** About 15% of the global population is Rh-negative, and scientists are not entirely certain of the evolutionary origin of the Rh-negative factor. Conspiracy theorists exploit this gap in knowledge to insert alien or supernatural explanations. Additional claims include that Rh-negative individuals have higher IQs, lower body temperatures, heightened psychic abilities, and a predisposition toward red or reddish hair.

**The "incompatibility" argument:** A key piece of the theory centers on Rh incompatibility in pregnancy -- when an Rh-negative mother carries an Rh-positive fetus, her body may produce antibodies against the baby's blood. Conspiracy theorists interpret this as evidence that Rh-negative blood is fundamentally "different" from normal human blood, paralleling the incompatibilities seen in animal hybrid breeding.

**Scientific reality:** Geneticists have explained that Rh-negative blood is a normal human genetic variation, no more mysterious than blue eyes or red hair. No reputable scientist or biblical scholar supports the alien or Nephilim origin claim. The Rh factor is well understood as a protein on the surface of red blood cells, and its absence is a straightforward recessive trait.

## 15. Loosh -- Energy Harvesting Conspiracy Concept

**"Loosh"** is a term coined by **Robert Monroe**, founder of the Monroe Institute and pioneer of out-of-body experience (OBE) research. In his 1985 book *Far Journeys*, Monroe described loosh as an energetic substance or life force produced by living beings through emotional experiences.

**Monroe's original concept** was neutral and exploratory -- he described loosh as a valuable form of energy generated by all life, not inherently negative. However, over subsequent decades, online communities reinterpreted his ideas into a much darker framework:

- **The "Loosh Farm" theory** claims that Earth is an energy-harvesting farm where higher-dimensional entities deliberately cultivate human suffering -- war, fear, grief, pain -- to feed on the emotional energy produced.
- **Archons:** Drawing from ancient Gnostic theology, the theory identifies these parasitic entities as "Archons" -- malevolent rulers of the material world who trap human souls in a cycle of reincarnation to perpetuate the harvest.
- **Prison Planet:** In the expanded theory, Earth is a "prison planet" where humans are involuntary energy producers, kept ignorant of their true nature by the entities that feed on them.

This narrative has gained significant traction on platforms like TikTok and Reddit, and has been connected to UFO whistleblower claims that "Earth is a cosmic farm where aliens harvest human misery."

Monroe's original work did not describe loosh as evil or humans as prisoners. The transformation from neutral metaphysical concept to fear-based conspiracy represents a significant distortion of the source material.

## 16. Ritual Sacrifice -- Conspiracy Claims

Ritual sacrifice conspiracy theories allege that political and economic elites participate in **occult ceremonies involving human sacrifice**. The primary focal point is **Bohemian Grove**, a private 2,700-acre campground in Monte Rio, California, owned by the Bohemian Club.

**What is documented:** The Bohemian Club is a real, exclusive private club founded in 1872. Its members have included U.S. Presidents, cabinet officials, corporate CEOs, and other powerful figures. The club holds an annual summer retreat at the Grove that includes a ceremony called the **"Cremation of Care"** -- a theatrical performance in front of a 40-foot concrete owl in which an effigy is burned, symbolically representing members' release from worldly concerns.

**The conspiracy version:** In July 2000, Alex Jones and cameraman Mike Hanson infiltrated Bohemian Grove and recorded the Cremation of Care ceremony. Jones characterized it as a "ritual sacrifice" and suggested the ceremony's effigy was not always symbolic -- implying real human sacrifice. This footage became foundational content for conspiracy communities. Jones' documentary *Dark Secrets Inside Bohemian Grove* (2000) inspired more extreme theories about the society.

**Assessment:** After decades of investigations, leaks, and infiltrations, no credible evidence has emerged of criminal or genuinely occult activity at Bohemian Grove. The Cremation of Care is a theatrical tradition. The verified facts -- that it is an exclusive retreat for powerful men with a somewhat eccentric ritual -- are unusual but not criminal.

Broader ritual sacrifice allegations also connect to claims about Satanic Ritual Abuse (SRA), which were widely investigated and debunked during the 1980s-90s "Satanic Panic."

## 18. Human/ET Hybrid Cloning -- Conspiracy Claims

The human-alien hybridization conspiracy theory claims that **extraterrestrial beings are conducting a secret breeding program** with humans, sometimes involving cloning technology, to create hybrid beings.

**Variations of the theory include:**

- **Alien abduction breeding:** Drawn from alien abduction narratives, the claim is that aliens (typically "Greys") abduct humans for reproductive purposes, creating hybrid offspring. Some proponents claim aliens are "in a reproductive bind on their home world" and use humans as surrogates.
- **Government collaboration:** A subset claims the U.S. government is a willing partner, allegedly stemming from a deal made after President Eisenhower supposedly met with Grey Aliens in 1954 at Holloman Air Force Base.
- **Underground facilities:** Theories about the **Dulce Base** in New Mexico allege that deep underground, "black-budget government scientists and extraterrestrial beings work side-by-side" on hybridization and cloning programs.
- **Replacement theory:** Some proponents claim the hybrid program aims to create beings that will "eventually replace humans as Earth's dominant species," with late-stage hybrids already integrated into human society.

**Scientific perspective:** Studies suggest that alien abduction experiences correlate strongly with sleep paralysis and lucid dreaming states. Research has shown that 75% of volunteers were able to reproduce the experience of alien encounters through induced lucid dreaming. No physical evidence of alien-human hybrids has ever been scientifically verified.

## 19. Reptilian Tankers -- Mind Control Gas Conspiracy

This entry combines two conspiracy theories: **reptilian aliens** and **chemtrails** (chemical trails sprayed from aircraft).

**Chemtrails:** The chemtrail conspiracy theory is the belief that the long-lasting condensation trails (contrails) left by aircraft are actually chemical or biological agents deliberately sprayed on populations. Alleged purposes include weather modification, population control, psychological manipulation, and biological warfare. The theory has been thoroughly debunked by atmospheric scientists -- contrails are water vapor that persists under specific humidity and temperature conditions. Harvard's Keith Group states plainly that the "chemtrails conspiracy theory" has no scientific basis.

**Reptilians:** The reptilian conspiracy theory was popularized by British author **David Icke** in his 1999 book *The Biggest Secret*. Icke claims that shape-shifting reptilian humanoids from the Alpha Draconis star system have infiltrated positions of power worldwide. These beings allegedly maintain human disguises while secretly controlling governments, financial systems, and media.

**The combined narrative ("Reptilian Tankers"):** On the Great Awakening Map, these theories merge into the claim that reptilian entities or their human collaborators use aircraft to disperse mind-control chemicals over populations. This represents one of the more extreme entries on the map, combining extraterrestrial conspiracy with atmospheric manipulation claims. The specific term "Reptilian Tankers" appears primarily in the most fringe conspiracy communities and does not have extensive mainstream coverage.

## 20. Smart Meters / RFID / 5G -- Technology Conspiracy Theories

This cluster groups three distinct technologies that conspiracy culture treats as components of a unified surveillance and control grid.

### Smart Meters
Smart meters are digital devices that record electricity consumption and transmit data to utility companies. Conspiracy claims include:
- **Health:** Allegations that the radio frequency emissions cause cancer, headaches, and other ailments. Scientific consensus holds that smart meter emissions are far below harmful levels.
- **Surveillance:** Claims that smart meters reveal intimate details of household behavior -- when residents cook, sleep, watch TV, or are away. This concern has some legitimate basis: researchers have demonstrated that smart meter data can identify specific appliances and behavioral patterns, and California utility companies have admitted to sharing data with government agencies and third parties.

### RFID Chips
Radio-Frequency Identification (RFID) technology uses electromagnetic fields to identify and track tags attached to objects. The conspiracy narrative centers on the **"Mark of the Beast"** from the Book of Revelation (13:16-17), which describes a mark on the right hand or forehead required to buy or sell. Since implantable RFID chips are typically placed in the hand and can be used for payments, some religious conspiracy theorists interpret them as a literal fulfillment of biblical prophecy. However, current implantable RFID chips do not have GPS tracking capability and can only be read at very short range.

### 5G
Fifth-generation cellular networks became the target of intense conspiracy theorizing, especially during the COVID-19 pandemic. Key false narratives include:
- **Immunosuppression:** Claims that 5G radiation weakens the immune system, making people vulnerable to COVID-19.
- **Virus transmission:** Claims that 5G towers actually transmit or "activate" the coronavirus.
- **Mind control:** Claims that 5G enables direct mind control, with COVID vaccines implanting receivers.
- **Real-world violence:** In 2020, these theories led to arson attacks on cell towers in the UK, Ireland, the Netherlands, Cyprus, and New Zealand.

Scientists confirm that 5G radio waves have no demonstrated harmful health effects and cannot transmit viruses or control minds.
