## 1. QAnon / Q / 17 -- Origin, Core Beliefs, "The Storm"

### Origin

On October 28, 2017, an anonymous user posting as "Q Clearance Patriot" appeared on the imageboard 4chan, claiming to be a high-ranking U.S. government official with Department of Energy Q-level security clearance. The first post asserted that the arrest of Hillary Clinton was "already in motion." The name "QAnon" combines the poster's pseudonym "Q" with "Anon," short for the anonymous users of the boards. The poster later migrated to 8chan (now 8kun), where the messages continued through December 2020, totaling roughly 5,000 posts known as "drops."

### Core Beliefs

The central claim is that a secret cabal of Satan-worshipping, cannibalistic pedophiles -- drawn from senior Democratic politicians, Hollywood celebrities, and business elites -- runs a global child sex-trafficking ring. Donald Trump is cast as the figure who was secretly recruited by military intelligence to dismantle this cabal. Followers believe a network of "white hats" inside the government is assisting Trump in this covert war against the "Deep State."

### "The Storm"

"The Storm" refers to a prophesied mass-arrest event in which thousands of cabal members would be detained, tried by military tribunals, and executed. The phrase traces to an October 2017 remark by President Trump while posing with military generals: "Maybe this is the calm before the storm." Q's first 4chan thread was titled "Calm Before the Storm." The Storm has been repeatedly predicted and has never materialized.

### The Number 17

Q is the 17th letter of the alphabet. QAnon followers treat the number 17 as a coded signal, reading significance into any appearance of the number in Trump speeches, jersey numbers, timestamps, or official communications. "17anon" has also been used as an alternative spelling to circumvent social media content moderation algorithms.

## 2. #WWG1WGA -- "Where We Go One We Go All"

### Meaning

WWG1WGA stands for "Where We Go One, We Go All." It is the most widely recognized rallying slogan of the QAnon movement, used as a hashtag, printed on merchandise, and displayed on banners at rallies (including January 6, 2021). It signals in-group solidarity -- the idea that all followers are "digital soldiers" united in a common cause.

### True Origin

The phrase comes from the 1996 Ridley Scott film *White Squall*, starring Jeff Bridges. The words appear on a ship's bell in the movie. Screenwriter Todd Robinson confirmed he wrote the line for the film; the bell was a prop made for the production and remains in the possession of Scott.

### The JFK Misattribution

QAnon lore falsely claims the phrase was engraved on a bell aboard President John F. Kennedy's yacht, the *Honey Fitz*. There is no historical evidence for this. The misattribution appears to have been constructed to give the slogan gravitas and connect it to JFK, a figure who features prominently in various conspiracy narratives.

### Use by Q

Q first used the phrase in a post in April 2018. It quickly became the movement's signature motto, appearing across social media platforms worldwide.

## 3. White Hats -- Concept in QAnon Lore

### Definition

In QAnon terminology, "white hats" are government insiders, military officers, and intelligence operatives who are secretly aligned with Donald Trump and working to expose and defeat the "Deep State" cabal. The term borrows from old Western movie conventions where heroes wore white hats and villains wore black hats.

### Role in the Narrative

White hats are the heroes of the QAnon story. They are believed to be conducting covert operations -- arrests, evidence-gathering, and information warfare -- against the cabal. Q is presented as one such white hat, leaking classified intelligence to the public through cryptic posts. The military, particularly figures associated with the National Security Agency (NSA) and U.S. Special Operations, are frequently cast as white hat forces.

### Broader Usage

Outside of QAnon, "white hat" is used in cybersecurity to describe ethical hackers who test systems for vulnerabilities with authorization. The QAnon usage repurposes this concept into a political-military narrative of good versus evil.

## 4. The Earth Alliance -- Conspiracy Concept

### Definition

In conspiracy theory circles, the "Earth Alliance" refers to a supposed coalition of nations, military factions, and secret societies working to overthrow a ruling global cabal (often called "the Cabal" or "the Illuminati"). Proponents allege that patriotic elements within the U.S. military quietly developed this international alliance to cut off the Cabal's financial supply lines, prevent World War III, and eventually expose the Cabal's crimes once it was weakened. ## 5. 4D Chess -- Trump Strategy Conspiracy

### Meaning

"4D chess" (also "3D chess," "5D chess," etc.) is an expression used -- sometimes sincerely, sometimes ironically -- to describe the belief that Donald Trump employs political strategies so sophisticated they are incomprehensible to ordinary observers. The implication is that moves that appear chaotic, contradictory, or self-defeating are actually deliberate steps in a master plan.

### Origin

The meme emerged in 2015-2016 during Trump's presidential campaign. Cartoonist Scott Adams (of *Dilbert*) published a blog post titled "2-D Chess Players Take on a 3-D Chess Master," praising Trump's persuasion skills. Users on Reddit's r/The_Donald amplified the concept, escalating it to "4D chess," "5D chess," and beyond as a humorous/serious expression of faith in Trump's genius.

### Role in QAnon

Within QAnon, 4D chess functions as an unfalsifiable explanatory framework. When events do not align with Q predictions -- when the Storm does not arrive, when arrests do not happen -- followers invoke 4D chess to argue that setbacks are actually part of the plan. This makes the theory resistant to disconfirmation: any evidence against it can be reinterpreted as evidence for it.

### Sarcastic Usage

Critics and skeptics also use "4D chess" sarcastically to mock the tendency to attribute strategic brilliance to actions that appear impulsive or poorly considered.

## 6. Anonymous -- Hacktivist Group

### Origins

Anonymous originated on the imageboard 4chan around 2003-2004. The name comes from the default username assigned to unregistered posters. Early activities were trolling and pranks, but the collective evolved into a politically motivated hacktivist movement.

### Key Operations

- **Project Chanology (2008):** Anonymous's first major public action -- a campaign against the Church of Scientology, including DDoS attacks, prank calls, and street protests where participants wore Guy Fawkes masks.
- **Operation Payback (2010):** Attacks on organizations opposing WikiLeaks, including PayPal, Visa, and Mastercard.
- **Arab Spring (2011):** Support for protesters through technical assistance and attacks on government websites.
- **OpISIS (2015):** Campaigns against ISIS social media accounts.
- **Ukraine Support (2022):** Cyberattacks against Russian government and media targets.

### Structure

Anonymous has no formal leadership, hierarchy, or membership. It operates as a decentralized, leaderless collective. Anyone can act under the Anonymous banner. Continuity exists only through shared interests, which broadly favor freedom of speech, opposition to government surveillance, and resistance to corporate power.

### Symbol

The Guy Fawkes mask from the graphic novel and film *V for Vendetta* became the group's iconic symbol.

### Connection to QAnon Culture

While Anonymous and QAnon both emerged from chan culture, they are distinct phenomena. Some early QAnon adherents borrowed Anonymous aesthetics, but the movements differ fundamentally: Anonymous is broadly anti-authoritarian and leftist-libertarian, while QAnon is pro-Trump and authoritarian in structure.

## 7. Meme Wars -- Information Warfare Concept

### Definition

"Meme Wars" (or "memetic warfare") refers to the use of internet memes -- images, phrases, videos, and slogans -- as tools of propaganda, persuasion, and political influence. The concept treats memes as weapons in an information battlefield where narratives compete for dominance.

### Theoretical Roots

The concept draws on Richard Dawkins's 1976 coinage of "meme" in *The Selfish Gene* as a unit of cultural transmission analogous to a gene. In the context of information warfare, memes become deliberately engineered cultural units designed to spread specific ideas or narratives virally.

### Real-World Examples

- **2016 U.S. Election:** Russia's Internet Research Agency (a troll farm) deployed memes across social media platforms to amplify pro-Trump and anti-Clinton narratives. Domestic meme campaigns on platforms like Reddit and 4chan also played a significant role.
- **Ukraine-Russia War (2022+):** Ukrainian government accounts used memes effectively to garner international sympathy and counter Russian propaganda.

### Role on the Great Awakening Map

The map places "Meme Wars" alongside "Anonymous" and "Psychological Thought Bombs" in the QAnon section, framing QAnon followers as participants in an information war against mainstream media narratives. QAnon adherents frequently describe themselves as "digital soldiers" in this memetic conflict.

### Military Interest

A 2015 NATO Strategic Communications paper discussed memetic warfare as a potential tool for shaping public opinion. The concept has been taken seriously as a vector for both offense and defense in information operations.

## 8. Space Force -- U.S. Military Branch + Conspiracy Theories

### The Actual Branch

The United States Space Force (USSF) was established on December 20, 2019, as part of the 2020 National Defense Authorization Act. It is the sixth armed service branch, organized within the Department of the Air Force. Its mission is to "secure our Nation's interests in, from, and to space." Responsibilities include satellite operations, space launch, surveillance of the space environment, satellite defense, and some missile defense functions. It was the first new military branch since the Air Force was separated from the Army in 1947.

The Space Force largely reorganized and elevated existing space-related missions previously scattered across the Air Force, Army, and Navy into a unified command structure.

### Conspiracy Theories

QAnon and adjacent communities have layered several conspiracy theories onto Space Force:

- **Election Monitoring:** A widely circulated theory claims Trump created Space Force specifically to use satellites to monitor and capture proof of fraud in the 2020 election. At Trump rallies, supporters have expressed belief that Space Force holds evidence that will overturn the 2020 results.
- **Solar Warden / Secret Space Program:** Some theorists claim Space Force is the public face of a decades-old clandestine space program (sometimes called "Solar Warden") involving advanced spacecraft and extraterrestrial contact.
- **Alien Technology:** Conspiracy narratives connect Space Force to the 1947 Roswell incident, claiming it will eventually reveal technologies reverse-engineered from alien craft.
- **Disclosure:** In the Great Awakening Map framework, Space Force is positioned as part of the apparatus that will facilitate "Full Disclosure" of extraterrestrial life and suppressed technologies.

### Official Response

The Space Force has attempted to distance itself from QAnon, including hiding QAnon-themed replies on its official social media accounts. In 2021, Space Force officer Matthew Lohmeier was removed from command after promoting political views and conspiracy theories associated with right-wing media.

## 9. End the Fed -- Federal Reserve Criticism Movement

### Historical Background

The Federal Reserve System, established in 1913, has been a target of criticism from its inception. Critics across the political spectrum have questioned its effectiveness in managing inflation, its role in economic crises, and its lack of direct democratic accountability.

Notable historical critics include Nobel laureate Milton Friedman, who argued that the Fed's policies worsened the Great Depression and who expressed a preference to "abolish the Federal Reserve and replace it with a computer."

### The Modern Movement

The contemporary "End the Fed" movement is most closely associated with former Congressman Ron Paul (R-TX). His 2009 book *End the Fed* debuted at number six on the *New York Times* Best Seller list. Paul argues that:

- The Federal Reserve is unconstitutional (no explicit Constitutional authority for a central bank).
- The Fed creates artificial economic booms and busts through monetary policy.
- The Fed enables excessive government spending and debt by monetizing it.
- The Fed erodes the purchasing power of the dollar through inflation.

The book's publication coincided with the 2008 financial crisis and the rise of the Tea Party movement, giving "End the Fed" mainstream visibility.

### Conspiracy Dimensions

In conspiracy culture, "End the Fed" merges with broader narratives about banking families (particularly the Rothschilds), the alleged illegitimacy of the 16th Amendment (income tax), and claims that the Federal Reserve is a private institution controlled by a secret elite. On the Great Awakening Map, "End the Fed" sits alongside "Follow the White Rabbit" and "17 Clock" in the QAnon section.

### Mainstream Economic View

Surveys of economists show overwhelming opposition to abolishing the Federal Reserve. Research from the Cato Institute and other institutions acknowledges Fed shortcomings while generally arguing that the solution is reform, not elimination.

## 10. Trust the Plan -- QAnon Slogan

### Meaning

"Trust the Plan" is a core QAnon slogan, repeated frequently in Q's posts. It functions as a reassurance to followers that current events -- no matter how discouraging or contradictory to expectations -- are unfolding as part of a controlled, predetermined narrative that will culminate in the triumph of good over evil. The exact nature of "the Plan" has never been specified.

### Function

The slogan serves several purposes within the movement:

1. **Cognitive dissonance management:** When predictions fail (arrests don't happen, the Storm doesn't arrive), "Trust the Plan" provides a framework for maintaining belief. Setbacks become tests of faith rather than evidence of failure.
2. **Authority reinforcement:** It positions Q as the holder of secret knowledge and asks followers to defer to that authority even in the absence of evidence.
3. **Community cohesion:** It functions as a shared article of faith that binds the community together.

### Related Phrases

Q also used "Enjoy the show," reinforcing the idea that followers were spectators watching a scripted drama unfold according to a predetermined screenplay. Other related slogans include "Patriots are in control" and "Nothing can stop what is coming."

### Cultural Impact

"Trust the Plan" became emblematic enough of QAnon to serve as the title of journalist Will Sommer's 2023 book, *Trust the Plan: The Rise of QAnon and the Conspiracy That Unhinged America*. Banners reading "Trust the Plan" were visible at the January 6, 2021 Capitol breach.

## 11. Red Pill -- Matrix Metaphor in Conspiracy Culture

### Origin

The metaphor comes from the 1999 film *The Matrix*. The protagonist Neo is offered a choice by Morpheus: take the blue pill and return to comfortable ignorance, or take the red pill and see reality as it truly is. Neo takes the red pill and discovers that the world he knew was a simulated reality controlled by machines.

### Adoption in Political and Conspiracy Culture

"Taking the red pill" or "being redpilled" has become widespread shorthand for what adherents describe as an awakening to hidden truths. The metaphor has been adopted by multiple communities:

- **Men's Rights / Manosphere (2000s-2010s):** The earliest political adoption, where "redpilling" meant accepting claims about female social privilege and feminist overreach.
- **Far-Right Politics (2010s+):** "Redpilling" expanded to mean accepting a range of far-right beliefs, including anti-immigration views, white nationalism, and deep state theories.
- **QAnon:** Within QAnon, redpilling specifically means awakening to the existence of the cabal and the secret war being waged by Trump and the white hats. "Red-pill the masses" appears explicitly on the Great Awakening Map.
- **General Conspiracy Culture:** More broadly, the term is used across conspiracy communities to describe any process of "waking up" to a supposed hidden truth that mainstream society denies.

### The Creators' Response

Lilly Wachowski, co-creator of *The Matrix*, has stated that the red pill was originally an allegory for the transgender experience, reflecting her and her sister Lana's journeys of self-discovery. Wachowski has expressed dismay at the co-optation of the metaphor by far-right movements.

### Criticism

Researchers note that "redpilling" creates insular communities where "truth" is presupposed rather than tested, contrary evidence is dismissed as propaganda, and the framework functions as a radicalization pipeline.

## 12. WTC7 -- Controlled Demolition Theory / 9/11 Truth

### What Happened

7 World Trade Center (WTC 7) was a 47-story skyscraper located north of the main World Trade Center complex. Unlike the Twin Towers, it was not struck by an aircraft on September 11, 2001. However, it was hit by debris from the collapse of the North Tower and burned for approximately seven hours. It collapsed completely at approximately 5:20 PM on September 11.

### The Conspiracy Theory

The "controlled demolition" theory alleges that WTC 7 was intentionally destroyed using pre-planted explosives. Proponents point to:

- The building's rapid, symmetrical collapse, which they say resembles a professional demolition.
- The fact that no plane hit it, making fire-induced collapse seem implausible to them.
- A remark by building owner Larry Silverstein, who said they decided to "pull it" -- which conspiracy theorists interpret as an order to demolish, while Silverstein says he meant to pull firefighting operations.
- Key advocates include David Ray Griffin (*The New Pearl Harbor*, 2004) and physicist Steven E. Jones.

### Official Investigation (NIST)

The National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) conducted a multi-year investigation and found:

- The collapse was caused by fires that burned uncontrolled for hours after the sprinkler system failed on lower floors. Thermal expansion of steel beams led to a cascade of structural failures.
- No evidence of explosives was found.
- The building took approximately 40% longer to fall than a free-fall collapse, inconsistent with controlled demolition.
- Controlled demolition charges large enough to bring down WTC 7 would have produced sound levels of 130-140 decibels audible up to 1 km away. No such sounds were recorded on any video.
- NIST determined it was "highly unlikely" that thermite could have been used to sever the building's columns.

### Cultural Significance

"Building 7" became a central rallying point for the 9/11 Truth movement. The phrase "WTC7 -- CONTROLLED DEMOLITION" appears on the Great Awakening Map in the Tribunals section, linking 9/11 conspiracy theories to QAnon's broader narrative of elite crimes.

## 13. "A Missile Hit the Pentagon" -- 9/11 Conspiracy

### The Theory

One of the most persistent 9/11 conspiracy theories claims that the Pentagon was not struck by American Airlines Flight 77 on September 11, 2001, but rather by a cruise missile or similar weapon, launched by elements within the U.S. government. Proponents argue:

- The hole in the Pentagon was "too small" for a Boeing 757.
- There was insufficient visible aircraft wreckage.
- No clear public video exists showing a large airplane approaching.
- The flight path and maneuver required would have been extremely difficult for the alleged hijacker, Hani Hanjour.

The theory was popularized by the 2005 French documentary *Loose Change* and Thierry Meyssan's 2002 book *9/11: The Big Lie*.

### Evidence Against the Theory

- **Physical wreckage:** Flight 77's black boxes, nose cone, landing gear, an airplane tire, and an intact cockpit seat were all recovered from the crash site.
- **Hole size:** The official ASCE Pentagon Building Performance Report estimates the impact hole was approximately 75 feet across, not the 16 feet widely misreported by conspiracy theorists. One wing hit the ground before impact and disintegrated; the other sheared off against the building's reinforced concrete columns -- neither was heavy enough to punch through the facade.
- **Eyewitnesses:** Dozens of people witnessed the plane approach and strike the Pentagon.
- **DNA identification:** All 64 people aboard Flight 77 were identified through DNA analysis from remains at the Pentagon.
- **Jet fuel:** Multiple survivors in the Pentagon's impact zone described the overwhelming smell of jet fuel.

### Structural Engineering Analysis

Professor Mete Sozen of Purdue University conducted simulations showing that the damage pattern was consistent with a large commercial aircraft striking at high speed, not with a missile impact.

## 14. The Philadelphia Experiment -- 1943 Alleged Teleportation

### The Legend

The Philadelphia Experiment is the name given to an alleged U.S. Navy experiment conducted around October 28, 1943, at the Philadelphia Naval Shipyard. According to the story, the Navy attempted to render the destroyer escort USS Eldridge invisible to radar. Instead, the ship allegedly became not only invisible but physically teleported from Philadelphia to Norfolk, Virginia (over 200 miles away) and back. Crew members were said to have suffered horrific side effects: some went insane, some developed mysterious illnesses, and some were allegedly fused into the ship's metal structure.

### Origin of the Story

The story surfaced in 1955 when a man named Carl M. Allen (also known as Carlos Miguel Allende) sent hand-written annotations in a copy of Morris K. Jessup's book *The Case for the UFO* to the U.S. Navy's Office of Naval Research (ONR). Allen followed up with letters describing the experiment. The story gained broader attention through Charles Berlitz and William Moore's 1979 book *The Philadelphia Experiment: Project Invisibility*.

### Debunking

- **Ship logs:** The USS Eldridge's deck logs show the ship was never in Philadelphia during the period in question. Records place it undergoing shakedown training in the Bahamas from September through December 1943.
- **ONR statement:** In 1996, the ONR officially stated it had "never conducted investigations on radar invisibility, either in 1943 or at any other time," and that the ONR was not even established until 1946.
- **Confession:** In 1969, Allen confessed that the entire story was a hoax, though he later recanted when new authors revived the story.
- **Degaussing:** The likely kernel of truth is that the Navy used degaussing equipment -- classified devices that scrambled ships' magnetic signatures to protect against magnetic mines and torpedoes. This mundane but classified technology may have generated rumors among observers.

### Cultural Legacy

The story inspired a 1984 film (*The Philadelphia Experiment*) and continues to circulate in conspiracy and paranormal communities. On the Great Awakening Map, it appears in the Tribunals section alongside the Montauk Project.

## 15. Montauk Project / Looking Glass

### The Montauk Project

The Montauk Project is a conspiracy theory alleging that the U.S. government conducted secret experiments at Camp Hero / Montauk Air Force Station on Long Island, New York. The alleged experiments encompassed:

- Time travel (a "porthole in time" and a stable "Time Tunnel")
- Teleportation
- Mind control and psychological warfare
- Contact with extraterrestrial life
- Dimensional portals

The story originated in a series of books by Preston Nichols, beginning with *The Montauk Project: Experiments in Time* (1992). Nichols claimed to have recovered suppressed memories of participating in the experiments. The narrative frames the Montauk Project as a successor to the Philadelphia Experiment.

### Evidence and Credibility

There is no evidence that the Montauk Project occurred. Psychologists have noted that "recovered memories" of the type Nichols described can be false memories shaped by suggestion. No documents, physical evidence, or corroborating witnesses outside the small group of claimants have ever surfaced. Nonetheless, the story has had significant cultural impact -- Netflix's *Stranger Things* drew inspiration from Montauk Project lore (the show was originally titled *Montauk*).

### Project Looking Glass

Project Looking Glass is a related conspiracy theory, popularized primarily by a figure calling himself "Dan Burisch" (claimed to be a microbiologist who worked at Area 51) in the early 2000s, and later amplified by a January 2012 interview with Bill Wood (Bill Brockbrader), who claimed to be a former Navy SEAL. The alleged technology is described as:

- A device reverse-engineered from extraterrestrial technology.
- A ringed apparatus that creates controlled distortions of space-time.
- A system that allows operators to view probable future timelines through a holographic projection.

## 16. White Dragon Society -- Asian Secret Society Claims

### Overview

The White Dragon Society (WDS) is a concept promoted primarily by Benjamin Fulford, a Canadian journalist who served as the Asia-Pacific bureau chief for *Forbes* magazine in the early 2000s before pursuing independent work focused on conspiracy theories and alternative geopolitical narratives.

### Fulford's Claims

Fulford claims to have been "the first Westerner in 500 years to be admitted into the ranks of the Eastern secret societies," which he collectively calls the White Dragon Society. According to his weekly newsletter (published behind a paywall), the WDS is a loose alliance whose members include:

- Eastern (Asian) secret societies controlling vast physical treasure
- European royal bloodlines
- "Gnostic Illuminati" leaders
- Military-industrial figures
- Russian FSB operatives
- Japanese military intelligence
- North Korean secret police

### Narrative

In Fulford's telling, the WDS is engaged in an ongoing covert war against a Western-dominated cabal (variously described as involving the Rothschilds, the Vatican, and sometimes non-human or "reptilian" elements). The WDS supposedly controls enormous gold reserves and is working to replace the current Western-dominated financial system.

### Credibility

Fulford's claims remain unverified by any independent source. No corroborating evidence for the existence of the WDS as described has been produced. Researchers classify his work as conspiracy-oriented narrative rather than journalism. On the Great Awakening Map, "White Dragon -- Asian Secret Society" appears in the QAnon/Tribunals section, reflecting its integration into the broader conspiracy ecosystem.

## 17. 17 Drops -- Q Posts

### What Are Q Drops?

"Q drops" (also called "Q posts" or "crumbs") are the messages posted by the anonymous figure Q on imageboards between October 28, 2017 and December 8, 2020. Approximately 5,000 drops were posted across three platforms: 4chan, 8chan, and 8kun.

### Characteristics

Q drops varied widely in format and content:

- **Cryptic questions:** Many drops consisted of leading questions rather than statements (e.g., "What if...?" or "Who controls [X]?"), inviting followers to construct their own interpretations.
- **Coded strings:** Some included alphanumeric strings presented as coded messages.
- **Predictions:** Many contained predictions about imminent arrests, revelations, or political events. Bellingcat's analysis documented that the vast majority of these predictions failed to materialize.
- **Slogans and rallying cries:** Phrases like "Trust the Plan," "WWG1WGA," "The Storm is coming," and "Where we go one, we go all."
- **Supposed insider information:** Claims about sealed indictments, military operations, and Trump's secret war against the cabal.

### The "17 Drops" Reference

On the Great Awakening Map, "17 Drops" is described as "High-Rank Military Intelligence," reinforcing the narrative that Q is a senior military/intelligence insider communicating through the drops. The number 17 is doubly significant: Q is the 17th letter of the alphabet, and QAnon followers note that Q's later posts (after an 18-month hiatus) were made exactly 1,700 days after the first drop, which they interpreted as deliberate confirmation of Q's identity.

### Aggregation and Interpretation

Q drops were collected by aggregator websites (such as qanon.pub and qmap.pub, both since removed) and interpreted by a community of influencers and decoders who translated the cryptic messages into specific conspiracy narratives.

### "Lost Drops"

Bellingcat's investigation revealed that early Q posts on 4chan were altered or deleted, undermining the mythology that Q's messages formed a coherent, unbroken record. These "lost drops" suggest the Q identity may have changed hands or that early posts were inconsistent with later narratives.

## 18. Save the Children -- Actual Organization vs. QAnon Co-optation

### The Legitimate Organization

Save the Children is an international non-governmental organization founded in 1919 by Eglantyne Jebb in the United Kingdom. It operates in approximately 120 countries and focuses on children's rights, education, health care, and emergency relief. The U.S. branch, Save the Children Federation, is headquartered in Fairfield, Connecticut. It is one of the world's most established and reputable child welfare organizations.

### QAnon Co-optation

In the summer of 2020, QAnon adherents co-opted the hashtag #SaveTheChildren on Facebook, Instagram, and Twitter to promote conspiracy theories about a global child sex-trafficking ring run by elite Democrats and Hollywood figures. The hijacking served several purposes:

1. **Recruitment:** The hashtag allowed QAnon content to reach mainstream audiences who cared about child welfare but had no prior exposure to QAnon. A Harvard Kennedy School study found that conspiratorial content posted under the hashtag was reposted at twice the rate of non-conspiratorial content.
2. **Evasion:** Using the hashtag helped QAnon adherents circumvent platform bans on QAnon-specific terms and accounts.
3. **Legitimacy laundering:** Associating QAnon's claims with a recognized charity's name gave the conspiracy theories an appearance of credibility.

### Impact

- **On the organization:** Save the Children Federation issued a public statement clarifying it was "not affiliated or associated with any of these campaigns" and that its name "in hashtag form has been experiencing unusually high volumes and causing confusion."
- **On platforms:** Facebook limited distribution of the #SaveOurChildren hashtag due to QAnon ties. Other platforms took similar moderation actions.
- **On real anti-trafficking work:** The National Center for Missing and Exploited Children and anti-trafficking organizations expressed concern that QAnon's baseless claims were overwhelming hotlines with false reports and diverting resources from actual cases.

### Street Protests

QAnon-organized #SaveTheChildren rallies were held in cities across the United States in August-October 2020. While many attendees were genuinely motivated by concern for children, the events served as on-ramps into the broader QAnon conspiracy ecosystem.
