# Great Awakening Map -- Secret Space Program & Related Topics

Research compilation covering 18 topics from the "Great Awakening Map" related to the Secret Space Program (SSP) conspiracy ecosystem. Each entry summarizes the claims made by proponents, provides historical context where applicable, and notes the evidentiary status.

## 1. Secret Space Program (SSP)

The "Secret Space Program" is an umbrella conspiracy theory alleging that one or more governments -- primarily the United States -- have been running covert space operations far beyond what is publicly known. Proponents claim these programs use reverse-engineered extraterrestrial technology, maintain bases on the Moon and Mars, and operate interstellar-capable spacecraft.

The theory's cultural roots trace back to a 1977 British television special called *Alternative 3*, originally broadcast as an April Fools' Day joke but subsequently taken literally by some viewers, spawning a persistent mythology. The modern SSP narrative was popularized primarily by Corey Goode and David Wilcock through the Gaia TV series *Cosmic Disclosure* beginning in 2015.

Core claims include: (a) secret military-industrial programs have developed antigravity and faster-than-light propulsion; (b) off-world colonies exist, staffed by personnel recruited through covert channels; (c) alliances and conflicts with multiple extraterrestrial species drive hidden geopolitics. No peer-reviewed scientific evidence supports any of these claims. The Pentagon's All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office (AARO) concluded in March 2024 that historical U.S. records show no evidence of hidden reverse-engineering efforts or extraterrestrial-derived craft.

## 2. Solar Warden -- Gary McKinnon Hack

![Gary McKinnon](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/3/34/Gary_McKinnon_cropped.jpg)

"Solar Warden" is the alleged name of a classified U.S. space fleet. The name entered public discourse through Gary McKinnon, a Scottish systems administrator who, between February 2001 and March 2002, gained unauthorized access to 97 U.S. military and NASA computer systems from London. U.S. prosecutors called it "the biggest military computer hack of all time."

McKinnon stated his motivation was to find evidence of UFOs, antigravity technology, and suppressed "free energy," inspired by the Disclosure Project's May 2001 press conference. He claimed to have found: (a) a spreadsheet listing "non-terrestrial officers"; (b) logs describing "fleet-to-fleet transfers"; (c) a reference to a program called "Solar Warden." He also described briefly viewing what he called "a silvery, cigar-shaped object with geodesic spheres on either side" in an unprocessed NASA image from Johnson Space Center (Building 8). McKinnon did not claim these were alien entities but suggested the U.S. military operates a secret space battalion.

McKinnon was indicted in the U.S. and faced extradition from the UK for nearly a decade. In 2012, UK Home Secretary Theresa May blocked the extradition on human rights grounds, citing McKinnon's Asperger syndrome and risk of suicide. He was never tried. No independent verification of his claimed discoveries has emerged.

## 3. TR-3B -- Alleged Triangular Antigravity Craft

The TR-3B "Black Manta" is a rumored triangular aerospace platform allegedly developed by the U.S. military under a classified black-budget program. It is often cited as an explanation for "black triangle" UFO sightings.

The primary source for TR-3B claims is Edgar Fouche, a former U.S. Air Force electronics specialist who served for 20 years and was stationed at bases near Area 51. In a 1998 presentation at the International UFO Congress, Fouche described the TR-3B as a triangular craft powered by a "Magnetic Field Disrupter" (MFD) -- a ring filled with mercury-based plasma pressurized to extreme levels and accelerated to high rotational speeds, creating a superconducting vortex that allegedly reduces the craft's effective mass by 89%.

Proponents link the technology to Operation Paperclip (the post-WWII absorption of German scientists) and to reverse-engineering of recovered extraterrestrial craft. The Belgian UFO wave of 1989-1990, in which approximately 13,500 people reported large, silent, low-flying triangular craft, is frequently cited as corroborating evidence.

In 2020, the U.S. Navy filed patents covering gravitational-wave generators and "spacetime modification" technology, which some interpreted as circumstantial evidence. However, holding a patent does not demonstrate a working device. Officially, the TR-3B does not exist in any acknowledged military inventory, and Fouche's claims have been challenged as a likely hoax by multiple researchers.

## 4. Dark Fleet -- Nazi Space Fleet Theory

The "Dark Fleet" conspiracy theory claims that Nazi Germany developed antigravity disc-shaped aircraft during World War II and, after the war, escaped to Antarctica and eventually established a secret space fleet. This fleet allegedly operates in coordination with a malevolent reptilian extraterrestrial species.

The theory was most thoroughly articulated in Len Kasten's 2020 book *Dark Fleet: The Secret Nazi Space Program and the Battle for the Solar System* (Inner Traditions). According to Kasten, Nazis and Reptilians infiltrated the U.S. military-industrial complex through Operation Paperclip, penetrating NASA and the CIA. The theory claims interstellar space ports exist in Antarctica and on Mars, and a base on the Moon. The Nazi-Reptilian alliance supposedly launched a secret space program featuring antigravity propulsion and hyperdimensional teleportation.

The theory draws on older narratives about missing U-boats, supposed Nazi bases in New Swabia (Antarctica), and an alleged SS Technical Branch responsible for developing flying discs. It also incorporates claims from SSP "whistleblowers" like Corey Goode, who describes encounters with a "Dark Fleet" faction during his alleged service.

Critical reviewers have noted the theory relies on dubious sources and has no basis in documented history. Historians of the Third Reich have found no evidence of antigravity research or Antarctic bases beyond a brief 1938-1939 German Antarctic expedition.

## 5. "20 & Back" Program -- Memory Wipe and Age Regression

The "20 and Back" is a central narrative device in SSP lore. It describes an alleged tour of duty lasting 20 years in a secret space program, after which participants are physically age-regressed to their original age, time-traveled back to the point of their initial recruitment, and have their memories wiped. They then resume civilian life with no conscious awareness of the intervening two decades.

The concept was first publicly described by Michael Relfe, who claimed to have served from 1976 to 1996 on a Mars base before undergoing the process. His wife, Stephanie Relfe, published his account in *The Mars Records* (2000), based on hypnotic regression sessions.

According to Corey Goode's account, the process involves: (a) completion of a 20-year off-world tour; (b) a two-week pharmaceutical and technological procedure during which the subject is physically immobilized; (c) physical age regression restoring the body to its state at recruitment; (d) temporal displacement back to the original departure point; (e) memory suppression, with "screen memories" implanted to fill the gap. Some individuals later recover fragments of these suppressed memories.

There is no scientific basis for age regression, temporal displacement of human beings, or the selective memory-erasure technology described. These claims exist entirely within the self-referencing SSP community and have never been independently verified.

## 6. Corey Goode -- SSP "Whistleblower"

Corey Goode (born c. 1970) is a central figure in the modern SSP narrative. He claims to have been identified as an "intuitive empath" at age six and recruited into a MILAB (military abduction) program, training from 1976 to 1986/87. In December 1986, he says he was recruited into "Solar Warden" under an Unacknowledged Special Access Program (USAP), assigned to a research vessel studying the solar system until December 2007.

Goode's key claims include: (a) a "20 and Back" tour of duty in space; (b) direct physical and telepathic contact with "Blue Avians," described as 8-foot-tall blue bird-like beings who are part of a "Sphere Being Alliance"; (c) visits to bases on the Moon, Mars, and various moons of the outer planets; (d) encounters with multiple extraterrestrial species and secret human factions, including the "Dark Fleet."

Goode rose to prominence through the Gaia TV series *Cosmic Disclosure*, co-hosted with David Wilcock from 2015 to 2018. His relationship with Gaia subsequently deteriorated, leading to a RICO lawsuit filed in 2020 (Goode et al v. Gaia, Inc., Case No. 1:20-cv-00742, D. Colo.). During litigation, Goode's claims were characterized by some observers as "creative intellectual property." A Colorado federal judge dismissed most of the claims in 2023 as too vague.

Former Gaia content head Jay Weidner publicly stated that both Goode and Wilcock exhibited "cult leader" behavior. No corroborating evidence for any of Goode's extraterrestrial or space-program claims has been produced.

## 7. William Tompkins -- SSP "Whistleblower"

William Mills Tompkins (1923-2017) was an American who worked in the aerospace industry and later claimed extensive involvement in a covert Navy program to build a secret space fleet.

Tompkins stated that in 1942, at age 18, he was recruited into naval intelligence at Naval Air Station, San Diego, where he served under Rear Admiral Rico Botta. His assigned role, he said, was to act as a "Disseminator of Naval Research and Information," carrying classified briefing packets -- distilled from debriefings of 29 Navy spies embedded in Nazi Germany's advanced aerospace programs -- to select think tanks, corporations, and university departments.

After the war, Tompkins worked at Douglas Aircraft Company (later McDonnell Douglas), where he claims to have designed spacecraft for the Navy's secret space program, including a 2.5 km spacecraft carrier and a 1.4 km battle cruiser. He stated that construction began in the 1970s and the first operational space carriers were deployed in the 1980s under "Solar Warden."

In December 2015, at age 92, Tompkins published his autobiography *Selected by Extraterrestrials*, detailing these claims. He appeared on *Cosmic Disclosure* and gave multiple interviews before his death on August 21, 2017, in San Diego.

Tompkins' verifiable career in aerospace is documented, but his claims about extraterrestrial contacts, spacecraft design for a secret fleet, and covert intelligence work go far beyond anything supported by publicly available records.

## 11. Dr. Steven Greer -- Disclosure Project

Steven Macon Greer (born June 28, 1955) is an American ufologist and retired emergency room physician. He holds a B.S. in biology from Appalachian State University (1982) and an M.D. from East Tennessee State University (1987). He practiced emergency medicine in Virginia before retiring in 1998 to pursue ufology full-time. He is also a former Transcendental Meditation teacher.

## 12. 1954 Eisenhower ET Meeting -- Holloman AFB

One of the oldest and most persistent UFO conspiracy narratives alleges that President Dwight D. Eisenhower met secretly with extraterrestrial beings on one or more occasions in 1954-1955.

The most common version of the story, popularized by lecturer Timothy Good, claims three meetings occurred:

1. **Edwards AFB (February 1954):** Eisenhower allegedly met with "Nordic" (tall, blue-eyed, human-looking) extraterrestrials who urged the U.S. to disarm its nuclear weapons in exchange for advanced spiritual knowledge. Eisenhower purportedly declined their condition of nuclear disarmament.

2. **Holloman AFB (February 1955):** Eisenhower allegedly met with a different group -- the "Grays" -- and signed a treaty. Reported terms included: the Grays could abduct humans for experiments provided they supplied names to the MJ-12 committee; the U.S. would receive advanced technology; and the existence of ETs would not be publicly disclosed.

The story points to a real historical anomaly: Eisenhower briefly "disappeared" during a Palm Springs vacation on the evening of February 20, 1954. His press secretary told reporters the president had needed emergency dental work, and a local dentist later confirmed the visit. Conspiracy theorists contend this was a cover story.

Approximately 300 people reportedly saw Air Force One at Holloman during the alleged 1955 meeting, but no photographic or documentary evidence has surfaced. The Eisenhower Presidential Library has addressed these claims, noting the absence of any supporting documents in presidential records.

## 13. Battle of Los Angeles (1942)

![Battle of Los Angeles, 1942](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/9/91/Battle_of_Los_Angeles_LATimes.jpg)

The "Battle of Los Angeles" refers to a real historical event on the night of February 24-25, 1942, less than three months after Pearl Harbor and one day after the Japanese shelling of Ellwood, near Santa Barbara.

**What happened:** At 2:25 a.m., air raid sirens sounded across Los Angeles County. At 3:16 a.m., the 37th Coast Artillery Brigade began firing anti-aircraft guns at reported objects in the sky. Over 1,400 rounds of 12.8-pound shells were fired. The barrage continued sporadically until 4:14 a.m. A total blackout was enforced. Five civilians died (three from car accidents during the blackout and two from heart attacks attributed to the stress). Shrapnel rained across the city, damaging buildings and vehicles.

**Official explanations:** Secretary of the Navy Frank Knox called it a "false alarm" the next day. Secretary of War Henry Stimson maintained that 15 planes had been involved, possibly operated by commercial entities for psychological purposes. In 1983, the U.S. Office of Air Force History attributed the event to "war nerves" likely triggered by a lost weather balloon, with the barrage self-perpetuating as shell bursts were mistaken for further targets.

**UFO interpretation:** The extraterrestrial hypothesis was not part of contemporary 1942 reporting. It emerged decades later, largely driven by a single retouched photo published in the *Los Angeles Times* showing converging searchlight beams that appear to illuminate an object. Some modern UFOlogists point to the event as early evidence of extraterrestrial visitation, though historians generally consider the incident a product of wartime anxiety and misidentification.

## 14. MJ-12 / Majestic 12

Majestic 12 (MJ-12, also Majic-12) is a purported ultra-secret committee of scientists, military leaders, and government officials allegedly formed by President Harry S. Truman on September 24, 1947, to manage all matters related to extraterrestrial contact and recovered alien technology, including the alleged Roswell crash.

**The documents:** In December 1984, television producer Jaime Shandera received an envelope containing a roll of 35mm film in his mail. When developed, it appeared to show a 1952 briefing document prepared for President-elect Eisenhower describing MJ-12 and its operations. Ufologists Stanton T. Friedman and Bill Moore worked with Shandera to investigate. In 1985, they found what became known as the "Cutler/Twining memo" in the National Archives -- a document referencing Majestic 12 and purporting to be from Eisenhower's assistant Robert Cutler to General Nathan Twining.

**Debunking:** UFO skeptic Philip Klass determined that Robert Cutler was out of the country on the date of the supposed memo. He also demonstrated that the Truman signature on the briefing document was a photocopy of a genuine signature from an unrelated October 1, 1947 memo to Vannevar Bush -- complete with identical accidental scratch marks. The White House and National Security Council denied any organization by that name had ever existed. The FBI's final assessment stated plainly: "The document is completely bogus."

Despite thorough debunking, MJ-12 remains a cornerstone of UFO conspiracy culture and has heavily influenced popular media, including *The X-Files*, numerous films, and video games.

## 16. USAP -- Unacknowledged Special Access Programs

Unacknowledged Special Access Programs (USAPs) are a real category within the U.S. government's classification system, but they have been adopted into conspiracy narratives with greatly expanded significance.

**The real framework:** The U.S. government maintains a hierarchy of classification: Confidential, Secret, Top Secret, and above those, Special Access Programs (SAPs). SAPs are divided into "acknowledged" and "unacknowledged" categories. A USAP is so sensitive that the government denies its existence entirely. Officials who are not read into the program are authorized -- and expected -- to state that it does not exist. Funding is concealed within broad budget categories or classified annexes. Even within a USAP, knowledge may be compartmentalized so that no single individual understands the full scope.

The term "Waived Unacknowledged SAP" (WUSAP) refers to programs where even standard Congressional oversight is waived, with notification limited to a small number of senior officials.

**The black budget:** The aggregate U.S. intelligence budget was approximately $100 billion (as of the most recent public figures). Significant portions are allocated to programs whose existence and purpose are classified. The Advanced Aerospace Threat Identification Program (AATIP), a $22 million program investigating UAP reports, was hidden within the Pentagon budget for years before its existence was revealed by the *New York Times* in 2017.

**In SSP conspiracy theory:** Proponents like Steven Greer use the real existence of USAPs to argue that reverse-engineering programs, antigravity craft, and extraterrestrial technology programs are hidden within this classification framework, beyond the reach of Congressional oversight or public scrutiny. Recent UAP whistleblower David Grusch has similarly alleged that specific USAPs hold recovered non-human craft.

## 17. MILAB -- Military Abduction Program

MILAB (a portmanteau of "military" and "abduction") refers to a conspiracy theory alleging that military or intelligence agencies kidnap civilians -- sometimes staging the abductions to resemble alien encounters -- for purposes including mind control experimentation, trauma-based programming, and recruitment into covert programs.

The concept was first systematically described by Dr. Helmut Lammer of the Austrian Space Research Institute in his 1999 book *MILABS: Military Mind Control and Alien Abduction*. Lammer documented cases where UFO abductees reported secondary kidnappings by humans in military uniforms, often involving unmarked helicopters, electromagnetic disorientation devices, underground facilities, and the implantation of tracking or control devices (typically behind the ear).

In the SSP narrative, MILAB is the claimed recruitment pipeline. Corey Goode, for instance, states he was identified as an "intuitive empath" at age six and entered a MILAB training program. ## 18. Rear Admiral Rico Botta

Rico Botta (c. 1890-1952) was a real U.S. Navy officer with a documented career. Born in Australia, he entered the U.S. Navy in 1908 and served for over four decades. During World War II, he held the position of Assembly and Repair Officer at Naval Air Station, San Diego, overseeing aircraft engine design and ensuring combat aircraft were repaired and updated. For this work he received the Legion of Merit. On January 11, 1946, he was retroactively promoted to Rear Admiral (effective June 30, 1943). He retired from the Navy in 1952.

**In SSP conspiracy theory:** William Tompkins claimed that in addition to his official daytime duties, Botta ran a covert nighttime intelligence operation involving 29 Navy spies embedded within Nazi Germany's most advanced aerospace programs, which were allegedly developing antigravity flying saucers. According to Tompkins, Botta tasked him with distilling intelligence from spy debriefings into classified briefing packets and delivering them to select defense contractors, universities, and think tanks.

Michael Salla has promoted these claims extensively through his Exopolitics website and books, arguing that FOIA-obtained documents confirm Botta's role. However, the documents in question confirm only Botta's conventional naval service record, not the extraordinary claims about alien technology or spy networks studying antigravity craft.

Botta occupies an unusual position in this ecosystem: he was an actual, documented military officer, lending a veneer of historical grounding to claims that are otherwise entirely unsubstantiated.
