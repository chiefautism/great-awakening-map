## 1. United States Space Force

![United States Space Force logo](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/3/3a/United_States_Space_Force_logo.svg)

The United States Space Force (USSF) was established on December 20, 2019, when President Donald Trump signed the National Defense Authorization Act for Fiscal Year 2020, creating the first new branch of the U.S. armed forces since the Air Force was separated from the Army in 1947 ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_Space_Force), [Department of Defense](https://www.war.gov/News/News-Stories/article/article/2046035/trump-signs-law-establishing-us-space-force/)). The Space Force was organized as a separate military service residing within the Department of the Air Force, structurally analogous to how the Marine Corps operates within the Department of the Navy ([Air Force](https://www.af.mil/News/Article-Display/Article/2046061/with-the-stroke-of-a-pen-us-space-force-becomes-a-reality/)). At its inception, approximately 16,000 active-duty airmen and civilians from the Air Force Space Command were reassigned to the new branch, and Trump appointed Air Force General Jay Raymond as the first Chief of Space Operations, a position that eventually gained a seat on the Joint Chiefs of Staff ([Space.com](https://www.space.com/trump-creates-space-force-2020-defense-bill.html), [Military.com](https://www.military.com/daily-news/2019/12/20/its-official-president-trump-has-signed-space-force-existence.html)). The USSF's actual mission centers on organizing, training, and equipping personnel to protect U.S. and allied interests in space, with core responsibilities including satellite protection, space domain awareness (tracking objects and threats in orbit), missile warning and tracking, satellite communications, and position/navigation/timing services such as GPS ([NPR](https://www.npr.org/2019/12/21/790492010/trump-created-the-space-force-heres-what-it-will-do), [SpaceNews](https://spacenews.com/trump-signs-defense-bill-establishing-u-s-space-force-what-comes-next/)).

The organizational structure consists of three field commands: Space Operations Command (SpOC, redesignated as Combat Forces Command in November 2025), Space Systems Command (SSC), and Space Training and Readiness Command (STARCOM), which are further divided into 16 mission Deltas and three Base Deltas ([Wikipedia - Structure](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Structure_of_the_United_States_Space_Force), [CSIS Aerospace Security](https://aerospace.csis.org/us-space-force-primer/)). Each Delta is organized around a specific function -- such as orbital warfare, space domain awareness, or satellite communications -- and is led by a colonel ([DefenseScoop](https://defensescoop.com/2025/07/25/space-force-launches-new-unit-structure-system-deltas/)). In 2025, the Space Force began activating "System Deltas" to integrate acquisition and operational functions, including Systems Delta 85 for Space Domain Awareness and missile warning, activated on August 8, 2025, with additional System Deltas for satellite communications, combat power, and position/navigation/timing following through October 2025 ([DefenseScoop](https://defensescoop.com/2025/08/19/space-force-system-delta-85-syd-plans/)). The service's members are called "Guardians," and as of fiscal year 2023, the Space Force operated on a budget of approximately $15.4 billion -- modest compared to the Army or Navy ([Air & Space Forces Magazine](https://www.airandspaceforces.com/space-force-established-as-trump-signs-ndaa/)).

Despite these straightforward military purposes, the Space Force has become a canvas for elaborate conspiracy theories, particularly within the QAnon and Secret Space Program (SSP) communities. The most prominent conspiracy claim holds that the Space Force is actually a "disclosure vehicle" for a Secret Space Program that has allegedly operated since the 1980s under the codename "Solar Warden," a supposed fleet of interstellar spacecraft patrolling the solar system -- a narrative popularized by Corey Goode on Gaia TV's *Cosmic Disclosures* beginning in 2015, for which no credible evidence exists ([Wikipedia - Secret Space Program](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Secret_space_program), [Hangar1 Publishing](https://hangar1publishing.com/blogs/ufos-uaps-and-aliens/space-force-conspiracies)). Another conspiracy thread, known as "Italygate," alleged that Italian military satellites were used to hack U.S. voting machines during the 2020 presidential election, switching votes from Trump to Biden -- a claim promoted through QAnon channels that Trump's own chief of staff Mark Meadows emailed to acting Attorney General Jeffrey Rosen, whose deputy Richard Donoghue responded that it was "pure insanity" ([Wikipedia - Italygate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Italygate), [Mediaite](https://www.mediaite.com/opinion/trump-revives-italygate-the-weirdest-2020-election-conspiracy-of-them-all/)). No credible evidence supports any of these satellite-election or SSP-disclosure theories; the Space Force remains a conventional military branch focused on the increasingly contested domain of Earth orbit and near-space operations ([CNN](https://amp.cnn.com/cnn/2019/12/20/politics/trump-creates-space-force/index.html)).

## 2. BRICS

BRICS is a geopolitical bloc originally comprising Brazil, Russia, India, China, and South Africa -- the acronym initially coined as "BRIC" in 2001 by Goldman Sachs economist Jim O'Neill to identify fast-growing emerging economies, with South Africa joining in 2010 to form the current acronym ([Council on Foreign Relations](https://www.cfr.org/backgrounders/what-brics-group-and-why-it-expanding)). In August 2023, at the Johannesburg summit, the bloc extended membership invitations to six additional nations: Argentina, Egypt, Ethiopia, Iran, Saudi Arabia, and the United Arab Emirates; by January 2024, Egypt, Ethiopia, Iran, and the UAE had officially joined, while Argentina under newly elected President Javier Milei declined, and Saudi Arabia remained ambiguous about its participation ([The Daily Economy](https://thedailyeconomy.org/article/brics-2025-expansion-de-dollarization-and-the-shift-toward-a-multipolar-world/), [Chicago Policy Review](https://chicagopolicyreview.org/2025/10/08/brics-and-the-shift-away-from-dollar-dependence/)). In 2025, the bloc welcomed ten additional partner countries including Indonesia, Turkey, and Nigeria, bringing the expanded group to represent approximately 45% of the world's population and 37% of global GDP on a purchasing-power basis, rivaling the G7 in economic weight ([Rio Times](https://www.riotimesonline.com/brics-2026-complete-guide/)).

Central to BRICS' stated ambitions is de-dollarization -- reducing dependence on the U.S. dollar in global trade and financial transactions. At the October 2024 summit in Kazan, Russia, members discussed BRICS Pay, a decentralized payment messaging system designed to facilitate transactions in local currencies as an alternative to the SWIFT system ([Egypt Oil & Gas](https://egyptoil-gas.com/features/brics-summit-2024-in-russia-advancing-de-dollarization-and-cooperative-initiatives/), [Colitco](https://colitco.com/brics-summit-2024-de-dollarization-reserve-currency/)). In January 2026, the Reserve Bank of India proposed linking the Central Bank Digital Currencies of member nations, with an "interoperability" plan set for the 2026 BRICS Summit agenda in India ([Rio Times](https://www.riotimesonline.com/brics-2026-complete-guide/)). The New Development Bank (NDB), established in 2014 and headquartered in Shanghai, serves as BRICS' multilateral lending institution -- modeled as an alternative to the World Bank and IMF -- funding infrastructure and sustainable development projects across emerging economies, while the Contingent Reserve Arrangement (CRA) provides a mutual financial safety net analogous to the IMF's emergency lending function ([CFR](https://www.cfr.org/backgrounders/what-brics-group-and-why-it-expanding)). Despite these initiatives, independent analysts note that de-dollarization has proceeded slowly: the dollar still accounts for roughly 58% of global foreign exchange reserves and dominates in commodity pricing, and BRICS members themselves have significant internal economic rivalries, particularly between India and China ([Hudson Institute](https://www.hudson.org/foreign-policy/how-counter-brics-preserve-global-dollar-dominance-zineb-riboua), [Investing News](https://investingnews.com/brics-currency/)).

In conspiracy theory circles, particularly those aligned with QAnon and the "Great Awakening" narrative, BRICS is frequently reframed as an "anti-Cabal alliance" -- a coalition of nations supposedly working to dismantle the Western financial system controlled by shadowy elites (variously identified as the Rothschilds, the Illuminati, or the "deep state") ([Tandf Online](https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/23254823.2024.2352455)). In this interpretation, Russia and China are cast as heroic opponents of a New World Order, and BRICS' financial initiatives are read as evidence of an imminent global currency reset that will destroy the Federal Reserve ([RedefiningGod.com](https://redefininggod.com/2014/09/the-new-multipolar-world-and-the-golden-age-theyre-not-what-you-think-they-are/)). Other conspiracy theorists take the opposite view, claiming that BRICS is itself a tool of the globalist elite, designed to create a controlled multipolar order that ultimately serves the same masters ([Golden Age of Gaia](https://goldenageofgaia.com/2016/06/29/lance-schuttler-alliance-taking-new-world-order/)). Both interpretations lack evidentiary support and tend to flatten the complex, often contentious geopolitics of a heterogeneous bloc whose members range from democratic India to authoritarian Iran, and whose internal disagreements on trade, borders, and strategy are well documented ([Al Jazeera](https://www.aljazeera.com/features/2023/8/22/can-brics-create-a-new-world-order), [CADTM](https://www.cadtm.org/The-BRICS-and-de-dollarisation)).

## 3. Operation Highjump

![USS Philippine Sea during Operation Highjump](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/d/d0/Antarctic_expedition_of_USS_Philippine_Sea_%28CV-47%29_1947.jpeg)

Operation Highjump, officially titled the United States Navy Antarctic Developments Program, 1946-1947, was the largest Antarctic expedition in history up to that point, organized by Rear Admiral Richard E. Byrd Jr. and commanded by Rear Admiral Richard H. Cruzen (with operational command under Rear Admiral Ethan Erik Larson), comprising 4,700 men, 13 ships (including the aircraft carrier USS Philippine Sea), and 33 aircraft ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Operation_Highjump), [Britannica](https://www.britannica.com/topic/Operation-High-Jump)). The expedition commenced on August 26, 1946, with primary objectives that Byrd himself described at a press conference: "primarily a military mission to train naval personnel, test ships, planes and the new helicopters under frigid zone conditions, develop techniques for establishing and maintaining air bases in Antarctic," with secondary goals of increasing knowledge of hydrographic, geographic, meteorological, geological, and electromagnetic conditions ([HistoryNet](https://historynet.com/operation-highjump-1946-47-expedition-to-explore-antarctica-from-the-air/), [Coast Guard Aviation History](https://cgaviationhistory.org/1946-operation-high-jump/)). The Central Group reached the Bay of Whales on January 15, 1947, where they constructed Little America IV on the Ross Ice Shelf, and extensive aerial mapping was conducted, including a two-aircraft flight to the South Pole on February 15-16, 1947 ([National Archives](https://unwritten-record.blogs.archives.gov/2017/09/27/operation-hi-jump-exploring-antarctica-with-the-u-s-navy/)). The expedition's greatest achievement was the acquisition of approximately 70,000 aerial photographs of the Antarctic coast and selected inland areas, providing invaluable mapping data ([USCS](https://uscs.org/learning-center/operation-highjump/), [Arctic Institute](https://www.thearcticinstitute.org/cold-cold-war-rear-admiral-richard-byrd-antarctic-expeditions-evolution-americas-strategic-interest-polar-regions/)).

The operation suffered four fatalities: three crew members (Ensign Maxwell A. Lopez, ARM1 Wendell K. Henderson, and ARM1 Frederick W. Williams) died on December 30, 1946, when the Martin PBM-5 "George 1" crashed on Thurston Island, with the other six crew members rescued 13 days later; a fourth man, Vance N. Woodall, died on January 21, 1947 ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Operation_Highjump)). The expedition ended in late February 1947 -- earlier than some had hoped but not abnormally so -- because the Antarctic summer was closing and thickening sea ice risked trapping the aircraft carrier for the winter, which the Navy could not afford ([War History Online](https://www.warhistoryonline.com/world-war-ii/operation-highjump-ufo-conspiracy-byrd-antarctica.html), [AllThatsInteresting](https://allthatsinteresting.com/operation-highjump)).

The conspiracy version of Operation Highjump centers on a March 5, 1947, interview published in the Chilean newspaper *El Mercurio*, conducted aboard the USS Mount Olympus by International News Service reporter Lee Van Atta, in which Byrd warned that the United States should adopt defensive measures against "hostile planes coming from the polar regions" ([Gralien Report](https://www.gralienreport.com/ufos/the-odd-exploits-of-admiral-byrd-bitter-reality-at-earths-end/), [Michael Heiser Foundation](https://michaelsheiserfoundation.org/articles/the-admiral-byrd-operation-highjump-myth/)). This statement, which in context referred to potential Soviet long-range bomber threats (a standard Cold War concern in 1947), was later mistranslated and reframed by conspiracy authors as evidence that Byrd encountered Nazi flying saucers or entrances to a Hollow Earth ([Logically Facts](https://www.logicallyfacts.com/en/analysis/double-check-operation-highjump)). Antarctic explorer Paul Siple, who participated in the expedition, later noted that the reporters aboard the Mount Olympus had significantly exaggerated various claims ([Michael Heiser Foundation](https://michaelsheiserfoundation.org/articles/the-admiral-byrd-operation-highjump-myth/)). The so-called "Missing Diary of Admiral Richard E. Byrd," which describes Byrd flying into a Hollow Earth and meeting its inhabitants, was not written by Byrd at all but by Walter Siegmeister, writing under the pseudonym Raymond Bernard, a known proponent of Hollow Earth theories -- no historian or archivist has authenticated any such diary ([Logically Facts](https://www.logicallyfacts.com/en/analysis/double-check-operation-highjump), [War History Online](https://www.warhistoryonline.com/world-war-ii/operation-highjump-ufo-conspiracy-byrd-antarctica.html)). The Nazi-UFO angle was further propagated by Ernst Zundel, a neo-Nazi publisher writing under the name "Mattern Friedrich," who blended Operation Highjump with elaborate myths of a Nazi Antarctic resurgence ([Medium - Other World Archive](https://otherworldarchive.medium.com/nazi-ufos-hollow-earth-and-operation-highjump-574c08bc9fb1)).

## 4. Neuschwabenland

The Third German Antarctic Expedition (1938-1939) was organized by Hermann Goring under the leadership of Captain Alfred Ritscher, an experienced Arctic navigator, and departed Hamburg on December 17, 1938, aboard the MS Schwabenland, a converted freighter carrying 82 crew members ([Wikipedia - German Antarctic Expedition](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/German_Antarctic_Expedition_(1938%E2%80%931939)), [Wikipedia - New Swabia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_Swabia)). The expedition's primary purpose was economic: Germany was the second-largest purchaser of Norwegian whale oil, importing approximately 200,000 metric tonnes annually for margarine and soap production, and the regime sought to identify a suitable location for a German whaling station in Antarctic waters to reduce dependence on Norwegian suppliers ([Polar Stories](https://polarstories.ca/that-time-the-nazis-claimed-a-chunk-of-antarctica-9249b23c1153)). Between late January and early February 1939, the expedition conducted seven survey flights using two Dornier Wal flying boats launched by steam catapult from the Schwabenland, capturing approximately 12,000 oblique aerial photographs across roughly 600,000 square kilometers of territory within the Norwegian claim of Queen Maud Land ([Wikipedia - German Antarctic Expedition](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/German_Antarctic_Expedition_(1938%E2%80%931939))). The surveyed territory was designated "Neuschwabenland" (New Swabia) after the German region, and metal markers with swastikas were dropped from aircraft to stake a territorial claim, though Germany never formally advanced this claim through international channels and forfeited it entirely upon its defeat in 1945 ([Cool Antarctica](https://www.coolantarctica.com/Community/antarctic-mysteries-hitlers-secret-base.php), [Westarctica](https://www.westarctica.wiki/index.php/New_Swabia)).

The expedition returned to Hamburg on April 11, 1939, having conducted oceanographic studies near Bouvet Island and Fernando de Noronha on the return voyage ([Wikipedia - German Antarctic Expedition](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/German_Antarctic_Expedition_(1938%E2%80%931939))). Critically, no whaling station was ever built, no permanent base was established, and the Nazis were present on Antarctic soil for only a few weeks at most -- the expedition was a reconnaissance survey, not a colonization effort ([War History Online](https://www.warhistoryonline.com/instant-articles/nazi-super-fortress-in-antarctica.html), [Raffery.com](https://raffery.com/nazi-base-in-antarctica-debunking-the-secret-base-myth/)). Although the Nazis did land on the Antarctic coast, they never stayed for more than a few hours at a time ([Cool Antarctica](https://www.coolantarctica.com/Community/antarctic-mysteries-hitlers-secret-base.php)).

The conspiracy theory of a secret Nazi Antarctic base -- variously called "Base 211" or "New Berchtesgaden" -- was first propagated by Hungarian author Ladislas Szabo, and there is no reference to either a submarine convoy or the "New Berchtesgaden" base before his publications; no evidence for either has ever surfaced ([Cool Antarctica](https://www.coolantarctica.com/Community/antarctic-mysteries-hitlers-secret-base.php)). The theory claims that fleeing Nazis established a fortress in Neuschwabenland at the end of World War II, complete with advanced weaponry and possibly flying saucers, and that Operation Highjump was actually sent to destroy it. Historian Colin Summerhayes debunked these claims in a peer-reviewed paper, demonstrating through declassified documents that the 1938-39 expedition left neither the time nor the resources to build any permanent structure, let alone a city-sized fortress ([History.co.uk](https://www.history.co.uk/articles/debunking-the-biggest-antarctica-conspiracy-theories), [Raffery.com](https://raffery.com/nazi-base-in-antarctica-debunking-the-secret-base-myth/)). All regions of Antarctica, including the area claimed as Neuschwabenland, have since been mapped, drilled, and surveyed by multiple nations over decades, and no Nazi base, wreckage, or artifacts have ever been found ([Malorie's Adventures](https://www.maloriesadventures.com/blog/the-secret-nazi-base-in-antarctica-truth-myth-or-conspiracy)).

## 5. Vril Society

The concept of "Vril" originates entirely from fiction: Edward Bulwer-Lytton's 1871 novel *Vril: The Power of the Coming Race* (originally published as *The Coming Race*), which depicts an unnamed American narrator who discovers an advanced subterranean civilization called the Vril-ya, descendants of humans who fled underground after a catastrophic flood and who control an "all-permeating fluid" called Vril -- modeled on the newly discovered force of electromagnetism -- through hollow rods capable of both destruction and healing ([Wikipedia - Vril](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vril), [SuperSummary](https://www.supersummary.com/the-coming-race/summary/), [Project Gutenberg](https://www.gutenberg.org/ebooks/1951)). The novel was a work of speculative fiction and social satire, with the plot serving primarily as a framing device for Bulwer-Lytton's commentary on Victorian society, technology, and gender relations; the Vril-ya civilization has no war because the destructive power available to every citizen makes conflict suicidal ([Victorian Web](https://victorianweb.org/victorian/authors/bulwer/comingrace.html), [Goodreads](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/1014436.The_Coming_Race)). Some contemporary readers, particularly Theosophists like Helena Blavatsky, accepted the novel as partially based on occult truth, but Bulwer-Lytton himself never claimed it as anything other than fiction ([Wikipedia - Vril](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vril)).

The claim that a "Vril Society" (Vril-Gesellschaft) existed as a secret occult group in Weimar-era Berlin was first made in 1960 by journalists Louis Pauwels and Jacques Bergier in their book *The Morning of the Magicians* (*Le Matin des Magiciens*), which alleged the society was an inner circle of the Thule Society that sought to harness the Vril force described in Bulwer-Lytton's novel ([Wikipedia - Vril Society](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vril_Society), [Wikipedia - Morning of the Magicians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Morning_of_the_Magicians)). The authors cited as partial inspiration a 1947 article by German rocket engineer Willy Ley, who had mentioned a Berlin group that sought the Vril force, but historians have shown that Ley's vague reference provided no substantive basis for the elaborate claims Pauwels and Bergier constructed ([Wikipedia - Vril Society](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vril_Society)). Critically, no primary source documentation -- no membership records, meeting minutes, correspondence, or contemporary press accounts -- for a "Vril Society" has ever been found in German archives; the authors never provided evidence for their "far-reaching speculations, not even for the alleged names of this group," and historians have classified their claims as fictions ([Wikipedia - Vril Society](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vril_Society)).

Historian Nicholas Goodrick-Clarke, the leading academic authority on the relationship between occultism and Nazism, addressed the Vril Society in his 2002 book *Black Sun: Aryan Cults, Esoteric Nazism, and the Politics of Identity*, finding the broader literature on occult-Nazi connections to be "rich in mystery and suggestion, but short on facts and hard evidence" ([Wikipedia - Black Sun](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Black_Sun_(Goodrick-Clarke_book)), [Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/Black-Sun-Esoteric-Politics-Identity/dp/0814731554)). His earlier work, *The Occult Roots of Nazism* (1985), rigorously distinguished between documented historical connections (such as the Thule Society's real but limited influence on early Nazi party members) and the speculative mythology that had grown up around them ([Wikipedia - Occult Roots of Nazism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Occult_Roots_of_Nazism), [Goodreads](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/742868.The_Occult_Roots_of_Nazism)). Despite the complete absence of historical evidence, Pauwels and Bergier's invention spawned an entire literary genre of "occult Nazism" that continues to this day, feeding into conspiracy narratives about Nazi flying saucers, Antarctic bases, and secret technology -- all of which trace back not to any documented historical society but to a 1960 book by two French journalists and an 1871 Victorian novel ([Wikipedia - Vril Society](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vril_Society), [Publishers Weekly](https://www.publishersweekly.com/9780814731246)).

## 6. Project Blue Beam

Project Blue Beam is a conspiracy theory originated by Serge Monast (1945-1996), a Canadian investigative journalist and conspiracy theorist from Montreal, who presented the theory at a conference in 1994 and published it in a text titled *NASA's Project Blue Beam* ([Wikipedia - Serge Monast](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Serge_Monast), [AllThatsInteresting](https://allthatsinteresting.com/serge-monast)). Monast alleged that NASA and the United Nations were collaborating on a four-step plan to establish a totalitarian New World Order by replacing all existing religions with a single New Age religion headed by the Antichrist ([Newsweek](https://www.newsweek.com/what-project-blue-beam-conspiracy-theory-erupts-over-drones-2001051)). Step One involved the creation of artificially triggered earthquakes at precise locations worldwide, which would uncover fabricated archaeological evidence designed to discredit Christianity, Islam, and other traditional religions ([KIMU](https://karlismyunkle.com/2025/08/21/project-blue-beam-nasa-holograms-and-the-apocalypse-theory/)). Step Two was a "gigantic space show" using three-dimensional holographic projections and sound, beaming images of religious figures -- Jesus, Muhammad, Buddha, Krishna -- into the sky, which would then merge into a single entity: the Antichrist ([AllThatsInteresting](https://allthatsinteresting.com/serge-monast)). Step Three involved "telepathic electronic two-way communication," using extremely low frequency (ELF) waves to make individuals believe they were receiving divine communication in their own language ([US News Desk](https://theusnewsdesk.com/project-blue-beam-fake-alien-invasion-theory/)). Step Four consisted of a "universal supernatural manifestation" using electronic means, including a simulated alien invasion of every major city to provoke nuclear retaliation, and the simulation of a rapture-like event through electronic "soul absorption" ([AllThatsInteresting](https://allthatsinteresting.com/serge-monast)).

NASA has never acknowledged Project Blue Beam as anything other than a conspiracy theory, and no evidence has ever surfaced to support any element of the plan ([Newsweek](https://www.newsweek.com/what-project-blue-beam-conspiracy-theory-erupts-over-drones-2001051)). Monast died of a heart attack in his home on December 5 or 6, 1996, at age 51, the day after being released from jail following a brief arrest; his followers claim his death was suspicious and allege he was assassinated using "psychotronic weapons" to silence him, though official records attribute his death to natural causes ([Wikipedia - Serge Monast](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Serge_Monast), [AllThatsInteresting](https://allthatsinteresting.com/serge-monast)). Monast's works continued to circulate on the internet, influencing later conspiracy theorists including American evangelical preacher Texe Marrs ([Wikipedia - Serge Monast](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Serge_Monast)).

In the decades since Monast's death, Project Blue Beam has evolved substantially from its original religious framework into a broader "false flag alien invasion" theory. Modern iterations tie the theory to advancements in deepfake technology, augmented reality, HAARP, and SpaceX launches, claiming that "the tools to fool the entire world already exist" ([KIMU](https://karlismyunkle.com/2025/08/21/project-blue-beam-nasa-holograms-and-the-apocalypse-theory/)). The theory experienced a major resurgence in late 2024 when mysterious drone sightings over New Jersey and New York were reported by thousands of witnesses and remained unexplained for weeks; conspiracy theorists, including celebrity Roseanne Barr, immediately linked the drones to Project Blue Beam, claiming they were a prelude to a staged alien event ([Newsweek](https://www.newsweek.com/what-project-blue-beam-conspiracy-theory-erupts-over-drones-2001051), [NBC News](https://www.nbcnews.com/news/us-news/mystery-deepens-surrounding-drone-sightings-conspiracy-theories-take-f-rcna184365), [NewsNation](https://www.newsnationnow.com/space/ufo/what-is-project-blue-beam-drone-conspiracy/)). Federal officials stated the objects did not pose a public safety or national security threat and were in some cases normal commercial air traffic mistaken for drones, but the episode demonstrated how Blue Beam has become a default conspiratorial framework for any unexplained aerial phenomenon ([Christian Action](https://christianaction.org/project-blue-beam-the-conspiracy-theory-reignited-by-drone-sightings/), [Axios](https://www.axios.com/2024/12/19/drone-sightings-nj-conspiracy-theories-lawmakers)).

## 7. Agenda 21 / Agenda 2030

Agenda 21 is a non-binding action plan of the United Nations for sustainable development, adopted by 178 governments at the Earth Summit (United Nations Conference on Environment and Development) held in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil, in June 1992 ([Wikipedia - Agenda 21](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Agenda_21)). The 351-page document addresses issues including poverty, health, housing, pollution, management of natural resources, and the role of various stakeholders in achieving sustainable development; it is explicitly non-binding, meaning no signatory nation is legally obligated to implement any of its recommendations ([Wikipedia - Agenda 21](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Agenda_21), [Earthbound Report](https://earthbound.report/2012/02/15/the-truth-about-agenda-21/)). Its successor, the 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development, was adopted on September 25, 2015, by 193 countries of the UN General Assembly, establishing 17 Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) with 169 associated targets and 232 indicators, covering issues from poverty elimination and clean water to quality education and climate action ([UN DESA](https://sdgs.un.org/2030agenda), [OHCHR](https://www.ohchr.org/en/sdgs/about-2030-agenda-sustainable-development)). Like Agenda 21, the SDGs are not legally binding: "countries are expected to take ownership and establish a national framework for achieving the 17 Goals," but implementation relies entirely on voluntary national action, and there are no enforcement mechanisms or penalties for non-compliance ([UN Sustainable Development](https://www.un.org/sustainabledevelopment/development-goals/)).

The conspiracy theory interpretation of Agenda 21 gained mainstream traction largely through Glenn Beck, who warned on Fox News that Agenda 21 was a "seditious" conspiracy to cut the world population by 85%, create "government control on a global level," and establish a "police state" leading to "totalitarianism" ([Media Matters](https://www.mediamatters.org/glenn-beck/beck-conspiracy-theory-uns-agenda-21-will-result-centralized-control-over-all-human-life), [SPLC](https://www.splcenter.org/resources/reports/agenda-21-un-sustainability-and-right-wing-conspiracy-theory/)). In November 2012, Beck published a dystopian novel titled *Agenda 21*, depicting a post-Agenda 21 America where citizens are confined to planned communities, children are taken from parents and raised in group homes, mating partners are assigned, and people recite pledges in honor of squirrels -- a work of fiction that many readers treated as predictive nonfiction ([Popular Science](https://www.popsci.com/environment/article/2012-12/what-does-climate-scientist-think-glenn-becks-environmental-conspiracy-novel/), [Daily Texan](https://thedailytexan.com/2012/11/26/glenn-beck-offers-a-dystopian-future-with-agenda-21-leaves-readers-with-more-questions-than-answers/)). In January 2012, the Republican National Committee adopted a resolution characterizing Agenda 21 as "destructive strategies for sustainable development" and strongly rejecting it; this language was incorporated into the party's 2012 convention platform, marking the moment the conspiracy theory entered official party politics ([Salon](https://www.salon.com/2012/03/15/is_agenda_21_a_u_n_plot_to_kill_the_suburbs/), [Coastal Review](https://coastalreview.org/2012/04/is-smart-growth-a-un-conspiracy/)). The actual documents contain nothing about forced population reduction, abolition of private property, or world government; they recommend voluntary, locally driven sustainable development practices such as clean water access, waste management, and conservation ([Daily Beast](https://www.thedailybeast.com/agenda-21-the-un-conspiracy-that-just-wont-die/), [Felix Dodds Blog](http://blog.felixdodds.net/2013/05/glenn-beck-does-not-understand-agenda.html)). The conspiracy interpretation fundamentally misrepresents a non-binding framework of development recommendations as a coercive blueprint for global tyranny, ignoring both the document's actual content and its complete lack of enforcement power.

## 8. Depopulation Agenda

The "depopulation agenda" conspiracy theory posits that global elites are deliberately working to reduce the world's population through vaccines, engineered pandemics, chemtrails, and other covert means, drawing on a network of selectively quoted documents and statements to construct its narrative. The most iconic physical symbol of this theory was the Georgia Guidestones, a granite monument erected in March 1980 in Elbert County, Georgia, by a man using the pseudonym "Robert C. Christian" (possibly identified by CNN investigative journalist Thomas Lake as Iowa physician Herbert H. Kersten, who died in 2005) ([Wikipedia - Georgia Guidestones](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georgia_Guidestones), [CNN](https://www.cnn.com/interactive/2024/02/us/georgia-guidestones-mystery-cec-cnnphotos/)). The monument's first inscription read "Maintain humanity under 500,000,000 in perpetual balance with nature," which conspiracy theorists interpreted as a direct statement of the elite's depopulation target -- implying the elimination of over 90% of the world's population ([NPR](https://www.npr.org/2022/07/06/1110210885/georgia-guidestones-monument-damaged-explosion), [Paste Magazine](https://www.pastemagazine.com/travel/georgia-guidestones/georgia-guidestones-conspiracy-and-bombing)). On July 6, 2022, the Guidestones were heavily damaged by a bombing and were subsequently demolished by local authorities; no suspects were publicly identified ([Wikipedia - Georgia Guidestones](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georgia_Guidestones), [Fox 5 Atlanta](https://www.fox5atlanta.com/news/mystery-surrounding-georgia-guidestones-explosion-remains-a-year-later)).

The most frequently cited modern "evidence" for the depopulation agenda is a misquoted statement from Bill Gates' 2010 TED talk titled "Innovating to Zero!" about reducing carbon dioxide emissions ([Poynter](https://www.poynter.org/fact-checking/2021/bill-gates-didnt-say-he-wanted-to-use-vaccines-to-reduce-the-population/), [PolitiFact](https://www.politifact.com/factchecks/2021/oct/11/blog-posting/bill-gates-didnt-say-he-wanted-use-vaccines-reduce/)). Gates stated: "The world today has 6.8 billion people. That's headed up to about nine billion. Now, if we do a really great job on new vaccines, health care, reproductive health services, we could lower that by, perhaps, 10 or 15 percent" -- referring to slowing projected population *growth*, not reducing the existing population ([Newschecker](https://newschecker.in/fact-check/bill-gates-2010-ted-talk-on-vaccines), [FactCheck.org](https://www.factcheck.org/2021/03/scicheck-video-targets-gates-with-old-clip-misleading-edit/)). Gates' extensively documented reasoning, consistent across decades of his public statements and the Gates Foundation's work, is that when child mortality drops (through better healthcare and vaccines), families choose to have fewer children because they no longer need to have many to ensure some survive -- a demographic transition well-established in population science ([Snopes](https://www.snopes.com/fact-check/bill-gates-vaccinations-depopulation/), [RMIT](https://www.rmit.edu.au/news/factlab-meta/gates-article-not-scrubbed)). Multiple fact-checking organizations have debunked the "depopulation through vaccines" interpretation ([Africa Check](https://africacheck.org/fact-checks/meta-programme-fact-checks/no-bill-gates-not-practising-population-control-through), [AAP](https://www.aap.com.au/factcheck/false-bill-gates-depopulate-with-vaccines-news-a-conspiracy-theory-classic/)).

The conspiracy theory draws more credibly on National Security Study Memorandum 200 (NSSM 200), also known as the "Kissinger Report," a real and declassified 1974 document prepared under the direction of Henry Kissinger at the National Security Council following orders from President Nixon ([Wikipedia - NSSM 200](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Security_Study_Memorandum_200), [State Department Historian](https://history.state.gov/historicaldocuments/frus1969-76ve14p1/d121)). The memorandum identified rapid population growth in 13 specific developing countries -- India, Bangladesh, Pakistan, Nigeria, Mexico, Indonesia, Brazil, the Philippines, Thailand, Egypt, Turkey, Ethiopia, and Colombia -- as a potential threat to U.S. national security and economic interests, and recommended that the U.S. promote population planning programs in those nations ([HLI](https://www.hli.org/resources/the-kissinger-report-nssm-200/), [Mercator](https://www.mercatornet.com/kissinger_turned_population_control_into_a_cold_war_weapon)). Originally classified, the document was declassified on July 3, 1989, and adopted as official policy through NSDM 314, signed by President Ford on November 26, 1975 ([Nixon Library](https://www.nixonlibrary.gov/sites/default/files/virtuallibrary/documents/nssm/nssm_200.pdf), [LegalClarity](https://legalclarity.org/the-kissinger-report-nssm-200-and-us-foreign-policy/)). While NSSM 200 is a genuine document recommending that the U.S. leverage its foreign aid to encourage population control in developing nations -- raising legitimate ethical concerns -- conspiracy theorists extrapolate from it to claim an active, ongoing, elite-driven campaign of mass extermination, a leap the document itself does not support ([Pravda USA](https://usa.news-pravda.com/world/2026/02/24/666598.html)).

## 9. FEMA Camps

The FEMA camps conspiracy theory holds that the Federal Emergency Management Agency (FEMA) maintains a network of secret internment camps across the United States, intended for the mass detention of American citizens following the declaration of martial law ([Wikipedia - FEMA camps](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/FEMA_camps_conspiracy_theory)). The theory draws its most substantive fuel from Rex 84 (Readiness Exercise 1984), a classified contingency plan that was very real: developed by Lieutenant Colonel Oliver North (then the NSC White House Aide and NSC liaison to FEMA) and John Brinkerhoff (deputy director of FEMA's "national preparedness" programs), Rex 84 envisioned the detention of up to 500,000 undocumented Central American immigrants and 4,000 American citizens designated as "national security threats" in the event that the president declared a National Emergency, with accompanying suspension of constitutional protections ([Wikipedia - Rex 84](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rex_84), [WSWS](https://www.wsws.org/en/articles/2012/12/06/brks-d06.html)). The plan was first discovered by the Christic Institute in 1984 and revealed in detail by reporter Alfonso Chardy in the July 5, 1987, edition of the Miami Herald ([We Are The Mutants](https://wearethemutants.com/2017/03/13/look-it-up-check-it-out-rex-84-and-the-history-of-an-american-conspiracy/), [Boot Camp & Military Fitness Institute](https://bootcampmilitaryfitnessinstitute.com/2022/04/28/what-was-rex-84/)).

Rex 84's existence entered the public congressional record during the Iran-Contra hearings on July 13, 1987, when Texas Congressman Jack Brooks asked Oliver North whether he had been assigned to work on plans for "continuity of government in the event of a major disaster," and specifically raised concern about "a contingency plan... that would suspend the American constitution" ([WSWS](https://www.wsws.org/en/articles/2012/12/06/brks-d06.html), [Wikipedia - Rex 84](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rex_84)). North was visibly stunned and turned to his lawyer; Senator Daniel Inouye, the committee chair, immediately stated that the question "touches upon a highly sensitive and classified area" and asked Brooks not to pursue the topic -- the committee never returned to the subject ([Wikipedia - Rex 84](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rex_84)). This exchange -- a congressman being shut down while asking about plans to suspend the Constitution -- became foundational evidence for FEMA camp conspiracy theorists, who argued it demonstrated that such plans existed and were actively being concealed ([Geopolitical Monitor](https://www.geopoliticalmonitor.com/us-fema-camps-emergency-intervention-020/)).

In 2010, former Minnesota Governor Jesse Ventura devoted an episode of his TruTV series *Conspiracy Theory with Jesse Ventura* (Season 2, "Police State") to investigating FEMA camp allegations, visiting facilities and interviewing witnesses who claimed that prison-like structures were being built for the internment of citizens during martial law ([Wikipedia - Conspiracy Theory with Jesse Ventura](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Conspiracy_Theory_with_Jesse_Ventura), [IMDB](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt1749045/)). The episode prompted U.S. Representative Steve Cohen to call for its removal from the air, calling it "an outrageous distortion and an outright lie" and "dangerous and irresponsible" ([SPLC](https://www.splcenter.org/resources/hatewatch/un-trutv-fear-vs-fact-venturas-conspiracy-theory/)). Key claims from the episode and broader FEMA camp theory have been individually debunked: what conspiracy theorists call "FEMA coffins" are actually standard burial vault liners (grave liners designed to protect coffins from settling soil); H.R. 645's sponsor, Rep. Alcee Hastings, stated the bill's purpose was "to improve humanitarian relief to individuals and families displaced by a hurricane or other natural disaster"; and a FEMA spokesperson told PolitiFact that claims of Halliburton contracts to build detention camps were "unequivocally false" ([SPLC](https://www.splcenter.org/resources/hatewatch/un-trutv-fear-vs-fact-venturas-conspiracy-theory/), [PolitiFact](https://www.politifact.com/factchecks/2021/sep/16/facebook-posts/no-covid-wasnt-planned-fema-kill-thousands-and-ope/)). Rex 84 itself was a real and troubling contingency plan, raising legitimate civil liberties concerns about government overreach, but the leap from a Cold War-era readiness exercise to an active, operational network of concentration camps awaiting American citizens is not supported by evidence ([SPLC](https://www.splcenter.org/resources/reports/fear-fema/)).

## 10. The Storm (QAnon)

"The Storm" is the central prophetic event of the QAnon conspiracy theory -- the long-prophesied day when Donald Trump would authorize the mass arrest, military tribunal, and execution of thousands of members of a supposed satanic pedophile cabal controlling global institutions, including prominent Democrats, Hollywood figures, and business leaders ([Wikipedia - QAnon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/QAnon), [CBS News](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/what-is-the-qanon-conspiracy-theory/)). The phrase originated on October 5, 2017, when Trump, standing with military leaders, told reporters "Maybe this is the calm before the storm" -- a remark that Q, the anonymous poster behind QAnon, adopted as the organizing metaphor three weeks later when the first "Q drop" appeared on 4chan ([Britannica](https://www.britannica.com/topic/QAnon), [NPR](https://www.npr.org/sections/inauguration-day-live-updates/2021/01/20/958907699/the-qanon-storm-never-struck-some-supporters-are-wavering-others-steadfast)). Q's very first post on October 28, 2017, predicted that "Hillary Clinton will be arrested between 7:45 AM - 8:30 AM EST on Monday - the morning on Oct 30, 2017" -- a prediction that did not come true, setting the pattern for all subsequent Storm prophecies ([Newsweek](https://www.newsweek.com/qanon-trump-march4-predictions-failed-1573739), [Raw Story](https://www.rawstory.com/trump-qanon-2650891369/)).

Over the following years, specific dates for the Storm were repeatedly predicted and repeatedly failed to materialize: December 5, 2018, and January 19, 2019, were cited for mass arrests that never occurred; the 2020 election was supposed to produce a Trump landslide followed by the Storm; January 20, 2021 (Inauguration Day) was predicted to bring the arrest of Joe Biden and the declaration of martial law; and March 4, 2021, was identified as the date Trump would be inaugurated as the "real" president under a pre-1871 legal framework ([Newsweek](https://www.newsweek.com/qanon-trump-march4-predictions-failed-1573739), [Daily Beast](https://www.thedailybeast.com/pro-trump-conspiracy-qanon-keeps-missing-deadlines/), [FiveThirtyEight](https://fivethirtyeight.com/features/qanon-has-become-the-cult-that-cries-wolf/)). When each date passed without incident, Q or QAnon influencers explained the delays as necessary for strategic reasons -- claiming "disinformation is necessary" or that "the plan" required patience -- and new dates were set, a pattern researchers describe as "moving the goalposts" ([GNET Research](https://gnet-research.org/2021/03/31/how-qanon-reacts-to-failed-predictions/), [Paste Magazine](https://www.pastemagazine.com/politics/qanon/qanon-trump-parler-gab-conspiracy-theories-inauguration-day)). The final Q drop appeared on December 8, 2020, consisting of a link to a video; Q has not posted since, and the account has been effectively silent for over five years ([Wikipedia - QAnon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/QAnon)).

With Trump's return to the White House in January 2025, some QAnon influencers experienced a revival, celebrating the incoming administration and creating fake emergency alerts claiming that "All deep state traitors are to report immediately to Guantanamo Bay detention camp for court martial via televised military tribunal" ([ADL](https://www.adl.org/resources/backgrounder/qanon), [The Conversation](https://theconversation.com/how-the-qanon-movement-entered-mainstream-politics-and-why-the-silence-on-epstein-files-matters-261316)). However, the movement has fundamentally splintered: QAnon as a structured movement "based around secret codes and clues and riddles doesn't so much exist anymore," having fragmented across platforms like Telegram, Rumble, and Truth Social, where its themes have been absorbed into broader conspiratorial ecosystems without a central organizing figure ([NPR](https://www.npr.org/transcripts/nx-s1-5230801), [Ohio Capital Journal](https://ohiocapitaljournal.com/2025/07/23/how-qanon-entered-mainstream-politics-and-why-the-silence-on-epstein-files-matters/)). The release of the Jeffrey Epstein files under Trump's administration, which conspiracy theorists had long demanded as the key that would unlock the Storm, produced no mass arrests or revelations of a satanic cabal, leading to what one researcher described as "awkward silence" from QAnon influencers who had predicted the files would vindicate the entire Q narrative ([The Conversation](https://theconversation.com/how-the-qanon-movement-entered-mainstream-politics-and-why-the-silence-on-epstein-files-matters-261316)). As of early 2026, no mass arrests have occurred, no military tribunals have been convened, and no elements of "the Storm" as originally prophesied have come to pass -- yet the concept persists as an article of faith among a diminished but dedicated following who maintain that the plan is still unfolding behind the scenes ([Cambridge University Press](https://www.cambridge.org/core/books/abs/social-science-of-qanon/future-of-qanon/7119D163FF8DB19F2BAD699B7C024A72)).
